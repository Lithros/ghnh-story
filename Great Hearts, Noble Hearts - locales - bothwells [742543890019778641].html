Though nominally a club, Bothwell's has none of the customary trappings of such an establishment. Occupying a three-floor manse on the Seine just past the Wall of Charles V, members are each assigned a private room, replete with waitstaff, musicians, and the finest culinary output of French gastronomy and viniculture. The intricate Baroque woodwork on every wall speaks to the height of architectural fashion, but as loud as the decor may be, the watchword of Bothwell's is discretion. Members enter and exit at all hours, never crossing paths with one another except by their own deliberate choice and prearrangement. The King himself is said to frequent the club on occasion, yet still the luminaries of Paris make it their refuge on all other days.
