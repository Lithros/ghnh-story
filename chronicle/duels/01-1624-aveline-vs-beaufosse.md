# Jean-Luc Aveline vs. Godefroy de Beaufosse

*March 1624 · the doorstep of Alida Demachy, Paris · Cause: rival suitors publicly declared for the same lady · sabre against cutlass*

The first duel of the game, fought in the first week of the first month, before either man had so much as joined a regiment. Two newcomers to Paris arrived at the same door with the same bouquet-borne intentions — and the campaign's five-year tradition of monthly bloodshed began.

*Editorial note: quoted text is verbatim from the game record; Discord @-mentions inside quotations are rendered as the character's name.*

## The quarrel

**Narrator** — "Godefroy de Beaufosse had his sights set on the more respectable of the Demachy twins, Alida. He had everything in order -- boots shined, shirt freshly laundered, and a gorgeous bouquet tucked under his arm -- as he made his way up the avenue towards her apartments. Much to his shock, he found none other before her door than Jean-Luc Aveline, similarly appointed for courting. The two men stared at one another with suspicion. Their intentions were as obvious as they were mutually exclusive. Alida, standing in the doorway, clutched her hands to her breast, awaiting to learn which man's love was strongest. Her sister interrupted preparations for her own courting to stare down from her own window, and a crowd begin to gather around the men."

**Narrator** — "Does anyone dispute that this is cause for a duel?"

*The crowd's answer came back with four crossed-swords reactions and not one dissenting voice.*

**Phillipe du Kaneda** — "This is not some Court of Solomon. The lady's heart cannot be divided. It can only be won through a test of courage."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "As newcomers and friends they arrived, but have thusly discovered they yern for the same fair maiden! Let them settle this with a duel, and let us all see who is the quality of man who will stand and deliver! Let them duel!"

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "Hear, hear! Be valiant, disappoint not the lady!"

*Du Kaneda backed his enthusiasm with coin:*

**Phillipe du Kaneda** — "/subtly taps a purse of 25 Crowns and points to Jean-Luc Aveline as his man for the first non-combatant interested in a spectator's wager/"

*No taker stepped forward before the blades came out, and the purse went back into his coat. Meanwhile Aveline eyed his rival's bouquet:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I do hope that is a bouquet of yellow roses or spiderwort! Judging your intent, they will soon be speckled crimson!"

**Pierre de Tête** — "say the word, draw your sword, screw your courage to the sticking place."

*De Beaufosse, a gentleman lately come from rougher country, answered the challenge late that night — and named a most rural weapon:*

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "A duel it must be! This is, of course, the only way to satisfactorily resolve any dispute of importance. I will have you know, Jean-Luc Aveline, that I have spent some time in more rural environs where tempers run high, and I am fully prepared to provide a commensurate response! Here, I draw my cutlass!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "We can commence!"

*In the reckoning of expertise, the Narrator ruled that Godefroy held the advantage. It would not help him.*

## The duel

**Narrator** — "the duel will proceed with turns being announced by me. after I announce, the players will share their actions for that turn, and I will deliver the results"

**Narrator** — "in addition to planning their moves, players are responsible for tracking their wounds received. remember, when you dip below 50% max endurance, you must include at least one rest in each 12-turn sequence"

**Narrator** — "when your endurance reaches zero, you are dead"

**Narrator** — "====== THE DUEL BEGINS"

**Narrator** — "Turn 1. Declare your actions"

**Narrator** — "Godefroy's slash is blocked!"

**Narrator** — "Godefroy's cutlass shatters!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Aaauaugh!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "How fragile these implements are!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "To think, that you wilt at the first rebuff! I should think the lady will be grateful"

**Narrator** — "Of course, you can also surrender at any time."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Conveniently, it was not in my plans to duel the lady herself!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "But this is not over between us."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I rest."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I rest."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I slash with my sabre!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I rest."

**Narrator** — "Jean-Luc's mighty slash inflicts 26 damage on Godefroy!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "This blood...is a familiar flavor..."

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "Throw not your life away; 'tis a precious gift indeed."

## The issue

*Six turns, one broken cutlass, one sabre-stroke. Godefroy de Beaufosse yielded on his feet, bleeding freely before the lady's door.*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Well fought, good sir"

**Narrator** — "Jean-Luc, increase your expertise by one"

*(Asked the next morning whether the point was general, the GM ruled: "Specific to sabers.")*

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "If only I could say the...same of the blacksmith's offerings."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I need to sit down."

**Pierre de Tête** — "Now that the duel has ended, I have brought the doctor"

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "There, there, sir. Bad craftsmanship is the bane of many a gentleman."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "well...fought Jean-Luc Aveline"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I suppose we should clear the grounds so that Mr. Aveline can continue his courting."

**Pierre de Tête** — "and I believe Herr Loafmann is also at the gate"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I have found that crossing blades with a man is sometimes the best way to get to his core"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "It seems a man's core is a messy place, full of fluids and stench!"

*Herr Loafmann was indeed at the gate — earlier that evening Pain Loafmann had rolled a 6 to court Armadine Demachy, the sister watching from the upstairs window. Both twins fell in a single night:*

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "The Demachy twins, taken out of reach so soon-!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "They are quite the pair!"

*And the victor's prize followed at once, in the courting channel:*

**Narrator** — "Lady Alida, having witnessed battle in her name, is prepared to receive Jean-Luc's courtship."

> 🎲 Jean-Luc Aveline rolls 1d6 — **6** (courting Alida Demachy: success)

**Narrator** — "Off they go, arm in arm, Jean-Luc's bloody saber the perfect memento of their first rendez-vous."

*The next afternoon Aveline was crowing on the streets:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Leon Mandelbrot, t'is true that I had a successful night at the tables of Red Phillip's, but after seeking the favor of mlle Demachy, I could have lost every hand at the club and still won the most important hand!"

*The rivals themselves parted friends. The very next day, standing ringside together at the Mandelbrot–Mercoeur duel — a far bloodier affair — they reflected on their own brief passage of arms:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Dear Godefroy, I am thankful our match did not have to come to that!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Yes, a duel such as this would admittedly have ended my short life!"

*And two days after the duel, the reconciliation was made formal on the streets:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Godefroy de Beaufosse, to make amends for our incivility almost a month ago now, I would like to buy your drinks at the Frog and Peach if you will have me. I am hosting Monsieur Mandelbrot at Red Phillip's the second week of April, but I will make time any other week to 'bury the hatchet' so to speak."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "A hatchet? Is such a barbaric weapon your choice for your next duel?"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "A gentleman must defend his honor with whatever implements are at hand! But no, I hope to receive regular training in a more elegant weapon once I have established myself within a regiment."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I heartily accept your offer, with no need to inter an axe of any size!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Name the week, and I'm your man!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Certainly! You will see me in the Frog and Peach in the 3rd week."

**The reckoning:** Godefroy de Beaufosse — surrendered, 26 endurance lost, one cutlass destroyed. Jean-Luc Aveline — untouched, +1 sabre expertise, and the hand of Alida Demachy won on the spot.
