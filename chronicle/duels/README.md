# The Duels — a primary-source edition

*The thirteen formal duels of Paris, 1624–1628, turn by turn — the quarrels that caused them, the programmed exchanges as narrated, and what the winners took and the losers paid, preserved verbatim from the record. Dueling in this world was fought with foresight, not dice: each man plotted his cuts, lunges, parries and rests in secret, and the designs collided like clockwork. The only dice were for thrown weapons — see duel IX for the one time in thirty-six that mattered.*

### [Jean-Luc Aveline vs. Godefroy de Beaufosse](01-1624-aveline-vs-beaufosse.md)
*March 1624*  
Two suitors meet at Alida Demachy's door with identical bouquets — Godefroy's cutlass shatters on the first blocked slash, and Jean-Luc walks off arm in arm with the lady, bloody sabre in hand.
**Outcome:** Aveline victorious untouched; Beaufosse surrendered after 26 damage and a shattered cutlass; Aveline won Alida Demachy's courtship on the spot (+1 sabre expertise).

### [Leon Mandelbrot vs. Etienne de Mercoeur](02-1624-mandelbrot-vs-mercoeur.md)
*March 1624*  
Nineteen turns of rapier against rapier at Adélaïde Blaise's door leave both suitors soaked in blood — and the lady rejects the victor for failing to kill.
**Outcome:** Etienne yields on turn 19 (77 damage taken to Leon's 54) and invites his conqueror to the Hunter's; Adélaïde rejects the bleeding victor, who also drops a social level.

### [Pierre de Tête vs. Jean-Luc Aveline](03-1624-de-tete-vs-aveline.md)
*April 1624*  
A new Dragoon major and a jumped-up Cuirassier private meet at the Frog & Peach, and a 52-damage failed parry teaches Jean-Luc Aveline what enemy regiments mean.
**Outcome:** Jean-Luc Aveline yields after losing 104 endurance (143 to 39); Pierre de Tête wins from atop a table, gaining 5 status and +1 sabre expertise.

### [Leon Mandelbrot vs. Etienne de Mercoeur — The Rematch](04-1624-mandelbrot-vs-mercoeur-ii.md)
*May 1624*  
The rematch of the century: 35 crowns on the line, Mlle. Caillat fainting in her window, and an oath sworn on a grandfather as the same two suitors skewer each other at a second lady's door.
**Outcome:** Leon Mandelbrot wins again — Etienne yields on turn seventeen after four 22-point wounds (88 endurance lost to Leon's 45); Leon gains a rapier expertise and wins Laurence Caillat "forever and a day"; Aveline pays Kaneda 35 crowns.

### [Clemens Charron vs. Jules Lavelle](05-1624-charron-vs-lavelle.md)
*November 1624*  
Jules Lavelle hosts his own insulter to dinner at Hunter's before the sabres come out — not knowing the peasant across the table has been paid in anonymous pouches to kill him.
**Outcome:** Clemens Charron wins — three identical lunges of 15 damage ("lightning can strike thrice") force Jules Lavelle's surrender on Turn Twelve; Clemens takes +1 Expertise, 100 crowns in secret hit money from Hercule Bonaparte, and declines the bonus to finish the job.

### [Curtis Sinnoch vs. Alphonse Devereux](06-1625-sinnoch-vs-devereux.md)
*January 1625*  
Two dozen pies against one elephant: Curtis Sinnoch's dagger misses Alphonse Devereux, hits Hannibal, and six privates die in the pastry-strewn stampede.
**Outcome:** Alphonse Devereux wins by surrender on turn four; Curtis takes 64 damage, his thrown dagger wounds the elephant, six privates are crushed, and Alphonse wins Octavie Vidal on the spot.

### [Curtis Sinnoch vs. Lucien Vaillancourt](07-1625-sinnoch-vs-vaillancourt.md)
*March 1625*  
A falcon eats a courting-pigeon out of the widow Brugière's sky-written name, and the dove-wielding newcomer's clavicle pays for it in splinters.
**Outcome:** Curtis Sinnoch wins in seven turns — a single 60-damage cut shatters Lucien's clavicle; Lucien surrenders at 30 endurance, and Curtis rolls a 6 to win Félice Brugière on the spot.

### [Curtis Sinnoch vs. Jules Lavelle](08-1625-sinnoch-vs-lavelle.md)
*September 1625*  
Jules publicly backs a knighted commoner over Curtis for the Foot Guards' Majority, and the "cane bonus" scandal ends in sabres before the whole regiment — then chocolate at Bothwell's.
**Outcome:** Curtis wins in ten rounds untouched; Jules surrenders with a slashed side and a cut thigh; Curtis gains 1 Expertise but loses 1 status for beating a friend, and the two reconcile over chocolate at Bothwell's.

### [Jean Rochant vs. Amand Baschet](09-1626-rochant-vs-baschet.md)
*January 1626*  
Twenty-three rounds of jump-backs, blocks, and lunch invitations over the stolen Adélaïde Blaise — until Jean Rochant threw his cutlass on snake eyes and the game recorded its first duel fatality.
**Outcome:** Amand Baschet killed instantly — thrown cutlass to the throat on a 2d6 roll of 1+1; Jean Rochant gains 1 expertise and 6 status points.

### [Curtis Sinnoch vs. Jules Lavelle — The Friendly Duel](10-1626-sinnoch-vs-lavelle-friendly.md)
*July 1626*  
A no-stakes sporting duel on Bothwell's lawn ends with Curtis's knee shattering Jules's teeth — and the proudest beaver-ivory dentures in France.
**Outcome:** Curtis Sinnoch wins at Round Thirteen; Jules Lavelle knocked unconscious, teeth shattered by a knee, later fitted with beaver-teeth dentures; Curtis gains 1 expertise, loses 1 status point.

### [Henri Toulouse vs. Guerinet Durand](11-1627-toulouse-vs-durand.md)
*January 1627*  
Guerinet steals Henri's mistress with the Twelve Days of Christmas, takes a sea-bass slap in the street, and unveils Le Ouroboros at Red Phillips — only to drop his sword in surrender an instant after Henri's beheading cut was already committed.
**Outcome:** Henri Toulouse wins; Guerinet Durand beheaded in the instant of surrender (+4 SP and +1 base Expertise to Henri; epitaph: "lost his head over a woman")

### [Robert d'Engaux vs. Pépin Bonheur](12-1627-dengaux-vs-bonheur.md)
*April 1627*  
A ring slipped onto Armadine's finger mid-date sparks the parry-heaviest duel of the campaign — teeth broken by a knee, a surrender, a faint, and a courtship won over the loser's unconscious body.
**Outcome:** Robert d'Engaux wins by surrender after 22 rounds (+2 SP, +1 expertise); Pépin Bonheur vomits and passes out at 13 endurance; Robert wins Armadine Demachy on a 6 minutes later; Pépin dies on campaign one month after.

### [Jean Shawinigan vs. Louis Laveuse](13-1628-shawinigan-vs-laveuse.md)
*January 1628*  
A time traveler with a neural link and a sabre meets the washerwoman's bastard over Adélaïde Blaise — won and lost in a day — and paints Red Phillips a perfect crimson circle.
**Outcome:** Louis Laveuse killed in eleven rounds (90 damage; "Father will be so angry"); Jean Shawinigan wins his line's first-ever duel and keeps the lady.
