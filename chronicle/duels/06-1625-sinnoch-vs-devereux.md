# Curtis Sinnoch vs. Alphonse Devereux

*January 1625 · the street before Octavie Vidal's apartments, Paris · Cause: rival suitors for the same lady · dagger against cutlass*

The most famous afternoon of the entire campaign. Two dozen pies, one elephant named Hannibal, a thrown dagger, an ill-timed sunbeam, and six crushed privates of the Musketeers — all in a duel that lasted exactly four turns and cost the loser far more in pastry than in blood. It was also the duel that ended a friendship: Curtis Sinnoch had personally sponsored Alphonse Devereux's entry into Parisian society, and it was Curtis's own introduction that put the young Marine captain within reach of the lady they would fight over.

## The quarrel

*The prize was Octavie Vidal, and Curtis had been after her for a month already. In December 1624 the Narrator had set his target:*

**Narrator** — "Curtis Sinnoch, roll to court Octavie Vidal (SL 13). You should be aiming for a 4."

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **2** (target 4: the lady is unmoved)

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, lady luck, she abandons me this month! To find out if I've been discreet...."

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **2** (discretion check: not caught)

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "(Thats 3 twos in a row... But at least Im not caught!)"

*So in January he came back with artillery: pastry. The Narrator set the scene in five messages that deserve to be read exactly as they arrived:*

**Narrator** — "Octavie Vidal has long been the belle fleur of Parisian ladies. Vivacious and witty, her knowledge of courtly politics and her family's intimate connections in the halls of power make her a prize that seems, to most, out of reach -- until now."

**Narrator** — "As she progresses down the boulevard for her morning constitutional, fur coat pulled close around her, Mlle. Vidal's keen nose detects the unmistakable aroma of fresh baking."

**Narrator** — "She turns the corner by her apartments to find, laid out on a few haphazard tables in the street, not one, not five, but two dozen pies, each boasting a new and more unique filling, with beautiful, flaky brown crust. Curtis Sinnoch stands behind, directing Musketeer privates to adjust this or that in the display, making everything just so."

**Narrator** — "Octavie laughs, a clear, crystalline sound, and reaches to partake of some gooseberry. Before she can place hands on the pastry, the ground rumbles, and the precarious table collapses, dumping pies on the ground."

**Narrator** — "'An earthquake?' asks one of the bewildered privates. But the rumbling is rhythmic, and accompanied with the sound of heavy breathing."

**Narrator** — "An enormous grey mass turns the corner, decorated only with the colorful tunic of the Royal Marines worn by the elephant's rider, Alphonse Devereux."

**Narrator** — "The pachyderm extends its trunk in practiced supplication."

**Narrator** — "The gentlemen stare one another down. The lady claps her hands in delight and pats the elephant. The elephant blinks...but will the gentlemen?"

*They would not. What followed was a masterpiece of escalating politeness:*

**Alphonse Devereux** — "Good day, friend Curtis Sinnoch! I did not expect to see you until next week."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, indeed Alphonse Devereux! Tis a surprise to see you as well! Unfortunately, I am currently quite busy, and have been preparing some arrangements! My apologies for not having more time, I gather you are just passing through. We shall pick up next week at Hunters with you as my guest? I'd love to hear what adventures you have had, and I hope to have my own share!"

**Alphonse Devereux** — "We shall share our tales next week, but for now, Hannibal and I are here to see the lovely Mademoiselle Vidal. Good day, Curtis Sinnoch."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, that is a shame, that. I've grown to enjoy your company. Perhaps that impressive beast of yours has flung a clod of something into your ears, and you were unable to hear me properly? You are just passing through - I've been preparing quite the day with the radiant Vidal, including a breathtaking trip along the Seine (though not as breathtaking as she!)"

**Alphonse Devereux** — "I am not passing through though. I am sorry to hear you misunderstood me."

**Narrator** — "Both gentlemen have refused to relent. The crowd begins hushed whispering. At this point there can be only one outcome. But who will demand it?"

*From the crowd, Jules Lavelle helpfully poured oil on the fire — reaching for the rawest wound available. Alphonse's father had been murdered only weeks before, one more victim of the killer then stalking Paris:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "Stab the rapscallion in the eyes, Sinnoch! The lady should be with a peer! Shouldn't you still be in mourning, you cad Devereux?"

**Alphonse Devereux** — "My father reveled in life, so I mourn him by living my life to the fullest, Jules Lavelle. I'll thank you to not speak ill of him."

**Jules Lavelle** — "I said nothing ill of the man. I'll never get to know him better. That's something we have in common."

**Alphonse Devereux** — "Enough of this! Curtis Sinnoch, friend though you are, I will not be dissuaded."

**Alphonse Devereux** — "/from a top Hannibal, I draw my cutlass"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "It pains me you chose this route, given that the fine lady likely wouldn't even have paid attention to you had I not introduced you to the fine Genlemen of Hunters. This is how you repay me for my generosity, asking naught in return? You may claim to be gentleman, but this shows your true colors, friend"

## The duel

*In the duels channel, the weapons were named. Alphonse kept the cutlass he had drawn from the elephant's back. Curtis's choice was a calculated insult — the commoner's blade, chosen to remind the Devereux family where they had come from:*

**Narrator** — "Alphonse Devereux has chosen the cutlass. Curtis Sinnoch, your weapon?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I shall fight with the dagger! This once erstwhile friend has been brought up while attending gatherings with me, and thus, I shall favor the weapon he and his father likely did before joining the Elite."

**Narrator** — "Alphonse has the advantage, and will submit at least 6 turns' worth of routines to start. Curtis will submit at least 12 turns' worth."

*Alphonse's expertise outstripped Curtis's by five points — enough to force two mandatory rests into every twelve turns of Curtis's plot. The Narrator could not resist rubbing it in: "(Alphonse, you need none. You're just that good.)"*

**Narrator** — "THE DUEL BEGINS"

### Turn One

**Alphonse Devereux** — "I rest?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I jump back!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Don't be afraid of the elephant, Sinnoch!"

### Turn Two

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I rest!"

**Alphonse Devereux** — "I slash."

**Alphonse Devereux** — "I did not want to come to this, Curtis Sinnoch!"

**Narrator** — "Alphonse charges forward, swinging his cutlass in great measured strokes. Curtis grunts as his shoulder suffers a cruel blow that deals 64 damage."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "You had your chance to stand down, Alphonse Devereux - you have made your decision, and I put this outcome to the grace of God and his Mercy!"

### Turn Three

**Alphonse Devereux** — "I rest."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I Throw!"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **5** (a thrown dagger hits on 1, 2, or 3: a miss — and worse)

**Narrator** — "Curtis's dagger goes wide, embedding itself in the elephant! It trumpets in surprise and outrage, and immediately begins stomping all over the pies."

**Alphonse Devereux** — "Hannibal!"

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **6**

**Narrator** — "6 privates are crushed to death trying to get the beast under control."

*The table's reaction to that single line of narration: five elephants, two trumpets, two skulls, two pies, two grimaces, and two boxes of popcorn.*

### Turn Four

*Curtis, disarmed, wounded, and standing amid the wreckage of two dozen pies and six of his own privates, surveyed what God's Mercy had wrought:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Bah! The damned sun in my eyes... Gods Mercy shows itself in strange ways... As the pies are ruined, as is my bloodlust. The day is yours, Devereux. Surrender"

**Narrator** — "The duel is finished. Alphonse is the winner!"

**Narrator** — "Alphonse, gain 1 base Expertise"

**Alphonse Devereux** — "Mercy indeed!"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Alas, my heart and my shoulder are both broken. I need time to remediate on this..."

## The issue

*The spoils went to the victor immediately — the Narrator called for the courting roll within the minute, with an adjustment unique in the annals of the game:*

**Narrator** — "Alphonse Devereux, roll to court Octavie Vidal. Even with elephant desolation, you should be aiming for a 2."

> 🎲 Alphonse Devereux rolls 1d6 — **2** (target 2: exactly enough)

**Narrator** — "The lady welcomes Alphonse into her apartment forthwith, advising him to mind the pies, elephant and otherwise."

**Alphonse Devereux** — "Mademoiselle Vidal!"

*Curtis's answer came by courier. He had promised Alphonse a guest's welcome at Hunter's before the elephant rounded the corner — and a Sinnoch does not go back on his word, however icily he keeps it:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Alphonse Devereux receives a letter from a courier a day or two later after their duel:
Capt. Devereux,
Hunter's has be notified of your attendance on the 4th week of January. A Sinnoch does not go back on their word, as their honor and worth as a gentleman rests upon it. I pray you learn the same some day.
-Maj. CS"

*And then, out in the streets, the loser turned his face toward the war:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Alas, this cold air, the coldness in my heart, I feel like fine Paris is freezing me proper! No! I shall not have any more of this stifling, numbing chill! I Shall Volunteer this month for the campaign, and hope a victory born of blood, sweat and tears helps to reinvigorate me!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Curtis Sinnoch, maybe we can hitch a ride to the front together? I must say, your ability to take a hit is staggering."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, Jules Lavelle! I appreciate the company, as I realize I may need to re-think those I once held as friends. The shoulder is healing up nicely - I appreciate your words. My father was very strict with our upbringing - even if our reflexes were shite - pardon me, less then desirable... the strength of an Sinnoch was nigh-legendary."

**Jules Lavelle** — "And do permit me to apologize for the brusque tone of my letter last month. You have shown yourself of better judgment than I had surmised."

**Jules Lavelle** — "If only your aim were as true..."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I no not if the sun bursting forth from cover was God's own mercy saving Devereux, or a punishment for my own ego. Regardless, time spent upon the battlefield - I'll reap what blood and sweat sows, and return a better and more hardened man, one fit for this City"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Though, had that dagger found its mark... it certainly would have killed him. I can only imagine Father laughing hearing an errant dirk causing an Elephant to rampage and kill 6 sodding privates!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Was it 6? I didn't bother to count. It was all sort of...mixed together."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Trodden Pies, Injured and Enraged Elephants, and the screams of dying peasantry soldiers - a horrifying, yet somehow appropriate metaphor for our brief stint here on Earth, eh?"

**Guy de Cavoye** — "Let's hope Alphonse Devereux put on a show for the mademoiselle to rival that of the first spectacle!"

**The reckoning:** Alphonse Devereux — victorious without a scratch on turn four, +1 base Expertise, and the favor of Octavie Vidal (SL 13), belle fleur of Parisian ladies, won on a roll of exactly 2 "even with elephant desolation." Curtis Sinnoch — surrendered, 64 endurance lost to a single cutlass stroke across the shoulder, one dagger lost to an elephant, one courtship lost to a friend he had sponsored into society, and a volunteer's berth on the winter campaign. Hannibal — one dagger wound, avenged sixfold. Six privates of the Musketeers — crushed to death in the line of pastry duty. Two dozen pies — total losses. *Curtis would not have to wait long for redemption: he was back in the duels channel the very next game month, sabre in hand, against Lucien Vaillancourt.*
