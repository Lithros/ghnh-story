# Curtis Sinnoch vs. Jules Lavelle — The Friendly Duel

*July 1626 · a manicured lawn at Bothwell's, Paris · Cause: none — sport between friends, to half-blood ("Bloodied") · sabre against rapier*

The tenth duel of the game, and the first fought without a quarrel of any kind. Ten months after Curtis Sinnoch and Jules Lavelle shed each other's blood over a Majority in the Royal Foot Guards, the two men had become fast friends — carousing partners at Bothwell's, the only two officers of the regiment left in Paris while their comrades marched on the summer campaign of 1626. So Curtis, freshly muscled from months of secret training, proposed a "friendly duel": a sporting exhibition on the lawns of the most exclusive club in Paris, to stop the moment either man was winded. No stakes, no insult, no lady. It ended with Jules Lavelle unconscious on the grass, his teeth scattered across the lawn by his friend's knee — and, some weeks later, with the proudest set of beaver-ivory dentures in France.

## The quarrel

*There was no quarrel. What there was, instead, was this:*

### "Curtis is SWOLE AF"

*April 1626. Curtis Sinnoch had vanished from society for months. When he reappeared, the reason was evident:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "*Few have seen Curtis the past months, not even at Bothwell's. He is seen briefly with the fair Felice, and his apparent absence is explained - Curtis is SWOLE AF*"

*(Out of character: "Converted 5 pts of EXP to 1 pt of STR." Remember that number.)*

**Jules Lavelle** — "Mon Dieu, it's full of gains..."

*In May, with the campaign season taking Jean Rochant, Marius Thibodeaux, and half of Paris off to war, Curtis invited his regiment to celebrate at his club:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, a glorious war campaign, and a great time to be back in Paris! My compatriots of the Foot Guards, would you care to join me at Bothwell's this last week of June? My pockets are rife with dirty lucre from the rebels, and a celebration is the best way to spend it!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "it would be my pleasure."

*June 1626 found the two of them at Bothwell's for Curtis's birthday, where Jules marked the occasion at the no-limit table:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "Happy birthday, Sir Sinnoch!" — "It is surely a delight to be a guest in these halls once again." — "Let's try out this no limit gaming table they have here.... I'll stake, ah, 9 bets of 224 each!"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "You madman! And Cheers!"

*(The madman walked away up 224 crowns and three status points — "What a rush!")*

### The challenge

*Then, in the street, the proposal — framed not as a grievance but as a compliment. The "less then ideal circumstances" Curtis refers to are the September 1625 duel the two fought over the Majority:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "You know, Jules Lavelle, the last time we had a duel under less then ideal circumstances, you were incredibly quick on your feet! Very defensively oriented, but I did not get to see your offensive abilities"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "What say you to a friendly duel? with an expected stop when either of us are winded. Something to keep our wits sharp and our reflexes sharper?"

*(The terms, stated out of character: a friendly duel, halting when either party drops below half Endurance.)*

*From somewhere offstage, Marius Thibodeaux — who knew perfectly well what five points of expertise converted to strength could do — supplied the stage whisper:*

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "one slash kills him. oh.. merde."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Shhh!"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle no thrown weapons, naturally. We want this to be respectful!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I accept, sir! Shall we put on a show at Bothwell's?"

**Jean Rochant** — "I for one would like to be there to watch!" — "A friendly duel shall be a marvel indeed!"

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "Perhaps you could send a few letters to the front during the bout so we are kept in the loop."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle indeed! I assume Bothwell's can arrange for a semi-public space for us?"

### Arrangements

*Curtis set the date and threw open the doors — to gentlemen of sufficient standing, at least:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle shall we do it the last weekend of the month? As always, I am happy to have your company. And for that matter, any gentleman of decent standing is welcome! My name is on the line, so I expect proper behavior, but yes guests may accompany! Who? I need to make sure to make arrangements so you aren't turned away at the door!"

*(Out of character, Curtis opened the guest list to anyone of Social Level 6 or better, at 14 crowns a head to toady along.)*

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "(Jean can't come, he's fighting in the summer campaign)"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, if only ArcticFox were here, I'm sure he would love it!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "This is acceptable to my schedule. I shall endeavor to impress my lady."

**Jean Rochant** — "*reading letters from his friends in Paris*"

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "You could always bet on the outcome via carrier pigeon.."

*Clemens Charron, at Social Level 5, applied a week too late — and got a needle-edged consolation prize:*

**Clemens Charron** — "Sir, I would be most grateful and joyous if I could join you at a chosen time during this coming month, as suggested!"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Clemens Charron Ah, if only you had said so sooner, I would have made arrangements! If this drudgery continues, maybe we could duel, hrm? It can give you an idea of what you can aspire to!"

**Clemens Charron** — "Ah, it has indeed been too long since I have dueled! It would be a refreshing exercise."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Clemens Charron glorious! Let us see what the next month entails - I am not at all opposed to some more sport!"

## The duel

*July 1626, on the grounds of Bothwell's:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "*Curtis steps out into one of the many manicured lawns of Bothwells, taking an offered Sabre from an attendant and nodding appreciatively at the blade's shine*"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "A lovely afternoon for a Duel, is it not so, Jules? I figure this is a good way to keep our wits sharp while our Countrymen are at the front! We need to stay able to protect our fair Paris, non?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I relish the opportunity to prove myself in front of so many worthies. I had heard my father might be in the crowd today, but alas, it appears not to be the case."

*The arms: Curtis with a sabre at Expertise 12 — backed by that new strength — against Jules with his regimental rapier at Expertise 14. On paper, the finest blade Jules had ever carried into a duel. On the lawn, a last check of the rules of engagement:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "Remind me, sir, this was to first blood?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I believe it was to Bloodied - neither of us want to see the other put down, but lets have a proper sport of it rather than simply blocking and jumping back!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Yes, sir!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "*Jules tries not to focus on Curtis's bulging biceps.*"

**Jules Lavelle** — "En Garde!"

**Narrator** — "With his free hand, Curtis grabs Jules's collar and pulls down, bringing his knee up to meet his opponent's descending chin. ## The issue

*The friendly duel to half-blood thus ended at Round Thirteen with one duelist insensible on the lawn. The final accounting from the Narrator, with the same penalty Curtis paid the last time he beat this particular friend:*

**Narrator** — "Curtis Sinnoch, increase your expertise by one and lose 1 status point."

*Curtis finished the exhibition having taken 14 damage in total — two rapier scratches he compared unfavourably to shaving accidents. Jules took a 32-point sabre wound, a knee to the jaw, and the loss of his teeth. The friendship, remarkably, took no damage at all. Two game-months later, in September 1626, the two were back to promenading together, and Curtis noticed something new:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "By the way, Jules Lavelle - your smile absolutely shines! What did you have installed there?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Oh, these teeth? I asked for the strongest they had. As with many things, a New World import has revolutionized dentistry. I am now the proud bearer of a mouthful of beaver's teeth!"

*In the same street conversation, Sir Hercule Bonaparte proposed a four-man single-elimination tournament of martial skill, purse of 750 crowns. The two veterans of the friendly duel gave answers that measured exactly what it had cost each of them:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Hercule Bonaparte ah, that I very much would enjoy! Jules and I had a lovely bout last month - I for one would certainly toss my hat in!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Sir Hercule Bonaparte, I like your thinking. I myself am barely healed, so my body could not stand it. But I should very much like to observe, and perhaps wager on the outcome!"

*(The tournament died for lack of entrants.) Curtis, for his part, went on speaking of the afternoon as one of the great successes of the social season — and trawling Bothwell's for his next sparring partner:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Why Hercule Bonaparte - quite the generous offer! I shall make sure to get us something! Why, Jules Lavelle and I have dueled on these grounds before - would you care to go toe to toe?"
