# Pierre de Tête vs. Jean-Luc Aveline

*April 1624 · the Frog & Peach, Paris · Cause: enemy regiments — a Dragoon major and a Cuirassier private under one roof · sabre against sabre*

The third duel of the game, and the first fought not over a lady but over regimental colors. In the first week of April 1624 both men put on a uniform: Pierre de Tête bought his majority in Grand Duke Max's Dragoons, and Jean-Luc Aveline — victor of the game's very first duel — enlisted as a humble private in the Archduke Leopold Cuirassiers. The two regiments despise one another, and by the code of the game, when members of unfriendly regiments meet, honor demands blood. Two weeks later, they met.

*Editorial note: quoted text is verbatim from the game record; Discord @-mentions inside quotations are rendered as the character's name.*

## The quarrel

*It began, as regimental quarrels do, at the recruiting desk. Week One of April, in the Cuirassiers' hall:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Jean-Luc Aveline, reporting for duty!"

**Narrator** — "The subaltern on duty hands you a small pamphlet detailing the history and standards of the regiment. You have been assigned to the 3rd Squadron, F Troop, under the command of Roland Hodin, a dashing and brilliant young man."

*And that same morning, across town in the Dragoons' mess:*

**Narrator** — "Pierre de Tête, you have been given command of 3rd Squadron. The captains leading E and F Troops report directly to you."

**Pierre de Tête** — "Captains, let's take this glorious squadron to WAR!"

*The new major and the new private crossed paths on the streets within the hour, all peacock civility:*

**Pierre de Tête** — "Ahh, how fair the month of April. I see new uniforms filling the streets and mine looks particularily dashing!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Mine may need to be washed regularly due to the amounts of excrement I shall be working with, but I rather like the look of my uniform as well!"

**Narrator** — "In the distance, someone whistles tunelessly."

*Two weeks later, Godefroy de Beaufosse brought Aveline to the Frog & Peach as his guest — straight into a Dragoons' celebration:*

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "To see the Frog and Peach again is a refreshment! To inhale its odeur is a balm."

**Narrator** — "Godefroy de Beaufosse enters with Jean-Luc Aveline at his side, ready for a good time. After they settle in, they notice, amidst a swirl of laughter and celebration across the club, Pierre de Tête being fêted by his fellow officers of Grand Duke Max's Dragoons, with Alphonse Devereux tagging along.

Before long, the Dragoons become aware of the new arrivals, particularly the new cavalry private. Their jocularity turns to sneers of disgust. The new major finds himself pushed forward by his comrades, volunteered to deliver a message of regimental disdain directly to the jumped-up Cuirassier."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Dear Jean-Luc Aveline, do you not agree? Here is a place where those men who appreciate life itself may gather, yet some may feel that drawing blood is the only source of enjoyment."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Normally, I would agree, but these gentlemen do insult myself and my new brothers."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Perhaps I am a bit sour after some losses at the tables of Red Phillip's, but I cannot abide this affront"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I do not want to insult you, Godefroy de Beaufosse, as I am your guest, but I also do not want to insult you by showing myself not as a man of honor"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "What would that say about you, if your guest is without honor?"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "We must uphold honor, that is certain."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "And this does appear to be an unruly bunch of fellows..."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I shall restore order as swiftly as I am able and allow us to continue our drinks in peace"

*In the duel channel, the Narrator set the stage — and needled the club chat:*

**Narrator** — "To set the stage for the duel. Pierre has advantage, high enough that Jean-Luc must add one mandatory rest routine into each sequence. When you've finished peacocking about in the club chat, name your weapons."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I draw my sabre and shout 'Glory to the Archduke Leopold's Cuirassiers!'"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I also draw my sabre as I prepare to defend the Grand Duke Max's Dragroons!"

*Then came a long wait — the Dragoons' champion was slow to the floor (see Table talk), and Aveline killed time in the club:*

> 🎲 Jean-Luc Aveline rolls 1d6 — **3** (*"#stretches his legs"* — his verdict: "Average")

*Told that heavy wounds would cost him a second mandatory rest per sequence on top of his disadvantage, Aveline answered:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Let us hope it does not come to that!"

*When Pierre finally rose from among his fellow officers, he opened with the insult he had been volunteered to deliver:*

**Pierre de Tête** — "What is this? Gentleman, has the odor changed somewhat?"

**Pierre de Tête** — "Dare I say, I smell, cowardice?"

## The duel

**Narrator** — "============== THE DUEL BEGINS"

**Narrator** — "Sabre v. Sabre. Declare your actions when ready"

### Turn One

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I block!"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I rest!"

### Turn Two

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I block!"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I slash"

**Narrator** — "Pierre's attack bounces away uselessly!"

### Turn Three

**Pierre de Tête** — "curses!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I block!"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I rest"

### Turn Four

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I parry!"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I cut"

**Narrator** — "Jean-Luc's unwise parry fails to turn aside Pierre's renewed attack. Jean-Luc receives 52 damage."

*Fifty-two damage in a single stroke — more than a third of Aveline's entire endurance, and the heaviest single blow yet struck in the game. The crowd felt it:*

**Pain Loafmann** (from the crowd) — "oooooOOO"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Zut alors!"

**Pierre de Tête** — "Zounds, that's quite a bit of blood you have there sir"

*Aveline, still hoping to salvage something from the wreck of his parry, appealed to the Narrator:*

**etirflita** (out of character) — "So I suppose I cannot riposte an unsuccessful parry?"

**Narrator** — "you suppose right"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I will concede that none of this blood is yellow, so should you care to surrender, I will be gracious"

### Turn Five

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I rest. And bleed."

**Pierre de Tête** — "I rest"

### Turn Six

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I rest."

**Pierre de Tête** — "I rest"

**Narrator** — "Please wait while Pierre submits the next sequence"

*During the pause, the taunting filled the ring — and across the club floor, Godefroy answered Pierre's opening insult about the odor of cowardice at last:*

**Pierre de Tête** — "*heavy breathing* Had enough sir?"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Return the favor of a blade's point!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "T'is a flesh wound. A large amount of flesh...."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I'm certain that flesh was not needed for any important health-related function."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "After all, a balance of humors remains the most well-understood and crucial element of health!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "So just keep all that blood in there"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Most of the damage is internal. That's where my blood is supposed to be anyway!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** (across the club) — "No, that was simply the smell of, er, a horse! Yes, a horse just entered the club and then immediately left, as it, er, forgot its hat elsewhere! Yes, that's it."

### Turn Seven

**Pierre de Tête** — "I rest"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I slash!"

**Narrator** — "Jean-Luc turns the tables back on Pierre, delivering a swift stroke that deals 26 damage."

**Pierre de Tête** — "*licks his own blood* ahhh, well done, well done"

### Turn Eight

**Narrator** — "TURN...EIGHT...."

**Pierre de Tête** — "I rest"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I rest"

### Turn Nine

**Pierre de Tête** — "I slash!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I cut!"

**Narrator** — "Trading blows back and forth, the combatants' sabers trace a wicked pattern in the air."

**Narrator** — "Jean-Luc takes 26. Pierre takes 52."

*Now it was Pierre's turn to feel the 52. Godefroy repeated his medical counsel:*

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Inside! It stays on the inside!"

### Turn Ten

**Pierre de Tête** — "Good advice *wheezing*"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I block"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "You seem to bleed red as well."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I rest"

### Turn Eleven

**Pierre de Tête** — "I rest"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I rest"

### Turn Twelve

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I rest."

**Pierre de Tête** — "I SLASH!"

**Narrator** — "Pierre leaps atop a table and swings his blade wildly, chopping another 26 damage out of Jean-Luc!"

**Narrator** — "Proper form for cavalry, after all."

**Pierre de Tête** — "Yield, sirrah! You've already redecorated the place!"

**Narrator** — "Please wait while Jean-Luc submits through turn 24 (10 total new actions minimum)"

*There would be no turn thirteen. With Aveline cut nearly in half three times over and facing another ten actions at double disadvantage, the voice of reason spoke from ringside:*

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I suggest calling it here - honor has been upheld and need not cost a life on this day!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I yield. Well fought, Pierre de Tête!"

## The issue

*Twelve turns, four wounds, one tabletop. Jean-Luc Aveline took 104 damage in three blows — by his player's own accounting, his endurance fell from 143 to 39 — while Pierre de Tête bled 78 of his own.*

**Pierre de Tête** — "Jean-Luc Aveline and to you sir, you fought with honor."

**Narrator** — "Pierre de Tête, for defeating a member of an enemy regiment, gain 5 status points"

**Narrator** — "Jean-Luc Aveline, for losing to a member of an enemy regiment, lose 2 status points"

**Narrator** — "Pierre, increase expertise by 1"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I should hope I still have enough vigor and humours for the mlle Demachy!"

*And with the regiments' honor discharged, the two men sat down to drink together amid the carnage:*

**Pierre de Tête** — "I suggest we call in the doctor and finish the brandy while he tends to our wounds. A little more blood won't hurt the chairs"

**Pierre de Tête** — "I can suggest a very elegant cane-maker"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I once purchased a canesword, but it became quite sticky in the summers."

**Pierre de Tête** — "Godefroy de Beaufosse, are you speaking of sugar cane?"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Why yes, that may be it!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Jean-Luc Aveline, I believe the usual prescription is to replace any lost fluids with drink, thus avoiding an imbalance."

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Though I should say you nearly had him!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I had to think of my mistress, who would be distraught if I were a corpse"

*The club itself did not come through unscathed:*

**Narrator** — "The proprietor's wife has collapsed, and he frantically waves a rag to cool her down from all the excitement."

*Out on the streets that night, Aveline's whole report of the evening was two words:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "*Bleeds profusely*"

*Followed, some hours later, by a private's hard-won wisdom:*

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "I plan to recover with a drink at Red Phillip's during the first week of May from wounds taken in April. I'd prefer to not run into any more GDM Dragoons! Who knew that by joining a regiment I would see combat before performing any regimental duties!"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "ah, but is that not the best way to learn, to know you can prove yourself on the battlefield! Blood spilt day is blood saved tomorrow"

**The reckoning:** Jean-Luc Aveline — surrendered, 104 endurance lost (143 → 39), −2 status points. Pierre de Tête — 78 endurance lost, +5 status points, +1 sabre expertise, and one thoroughly redecorated gentlemen's club.
