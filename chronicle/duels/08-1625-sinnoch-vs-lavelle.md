# Curtis Sinnoch vs. Jules Lavelle

*September 1625 · the street before the Royal Foot Guards' barracks, Paris · Cause: Jules's public backing of Sir Hercule Bonaparte over Curtis for the Majority of the Royal Foot Guards · sabre against sabre*

The eighth duel of the game, and the first fought between men of the same regiment — indeed, between men who had been in the same regiment for less than a day. On a single September morning, three ambitious officers presented themselves at the Royal Foot Guards' barracks: Jules Lavelle took a Captaincy, and Curtis Sinnoch and the newly knighted commoner Sir Hercule Bonaparte collided over the one available Majority. What followed was the most naked influence war the game had yet seen — a recruiting officer with a perfumed letter in his vest inventing a "cane bonus" on the spot, a Captain publicly talking down a fellow noble's leadership to the commander's face, and a family so offended it had the recruiting officer exiled to the Frontier by nightfall. The duel itself ran ten rounds before the assembled Foot Guards, cost Jules a slashed side and a cut thigh, cost Curtis a status point "for beating a friend" — and ended, weeks later, with the two men sharing chocolate at Bothwell's.

## The quarrel

### The cane bonus

*September 1625. Jules Lavelle arrived at the Royal Foot Guards first, rolled exactly what he needed, and bought his way in:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "A simple matter. I'm something of a legacy."

> 🎲 Jules Lavelle rolls 1d6 — **4** (join the Royal Foot Guards; target 4: accepted)

**Jules Lavelle** — "I shall purchase for myself...a Captaincy." — "A step down, yes. But a lower rung on a taller ladder."

**Narrator** — "Curtis Sinnoch and Hercule Bonaparte arrive at the Royal Foot Guards' barracks early the same morning. Each is seen to breathe a sigh of relief when Jules accepts his Captaincy. But their Major's epaulettes gleam golden."

*Only one Majority was open. Hercule rolled first — and fell short:*

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 1d6 — **3** (join the Royal Foot Guards; target 4: turned away)

**Narrator** — "Hercule has been turned away. The Royal Foot are interested in those with pedigree."

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "Fortunate for Hercule that Alida is an old family friend of the recruiter" — "The benefit of Hercule's beau is done without his knowledge. In his mind, this application is on merit and talent alone."

**Narrator** — "As Hercule limps away from the command office, Germain Simond, the acting commander, flings the door open. 'Wait there!' He shouts. 'I forgot the...ah...the cane bonus. Yes. The cane adds dignity. The Royal Foot Guards...prize...dignity. Come back in, Sir!' He hurriedly tucks a perfumed letter back into his vest pocket."

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "*A perplexed Hercule returns to office... subtlety adding a small flourish to the movement of his cane, still somewhat confused.*"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, well, a cane bonus!  That, of course, cannot be understated!" — "But, what is a cane against the military might and family name of the Sinnochs!"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **2** (join the Royal Foot Guards; turned away — a level 5 favour needed to best Hercule)

**Narrator** — "Acting Commander Simond begins to sweat. 'Ah, Major Sinnoch...yes, of course, your family name precedes you. Pedigree, indeed!' He extends a Major's commission letter to Curtis."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, very good, my man!  It will be a pleasure following in my fathers footsteps!"

### "Wait a moment, sir."

*The commission was in Curtis's reach when the new Captain intervened — openly, before both applicants:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "Wait a moment, sir."

**Jules Lavelle** — "I may be a mere Captain in this regiment. But I have experience in leadership. And I can say, between these two, the Knight is the more reliable, the better acquainted with the integrity befitting a gentleman."

**Narrator** — "Jules has used a level 3 favour to influence the commander in favor of Hercule."

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "*Hercule looks up from his cane in mild shock, pausing his mumblings of congratulations to Maj Sinnock and trying to process the events unfolding before him*"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "What is that, my good Lavelle?  I understand you may have your opinions sir, and Good Hercule has certainly proved his place within the upper crust by deeds and actions - how has your own military 'leadership' faired you this past year?  I myself have been mentioned in dispatches no less then 3 times, and the stories of my valor are second to none!"

*Curtis spent his own last level 3 favour to answer. And then Hercule — the man being fought over — objected to the word "Good":*

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "*The unfolding competition kicks Hercule's mind back into gear. He straightens his back and flourishes his cane striking on the floor to demand the room's attention*

Maj. Sinnoch, while no one would deny your accomplishments to France, I believe in your boasting you may have forgotten yourself a bit.  I too have been mentioned no less than three times in dispatches and, as for valor... well... it is SIR Hercule, not 'Good' Hercule for a reason.

I understand that we both covet a Majority in this Regiment, but I take great pride in the day the King himself knighted me.  I ask that you not forgot it or gloss over it in front of the Commander here."

**Narrator** — "The two are currently tied. The tie will go to Hercule unless Curtis has more influence to spare."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Sir Bonaparte, you are correct regarding your title - my apologies for my slip up.  It is a new title which you have fully earned, and in no way was I speaking against your merits - more of Lavelle here butting in"

**Narrator** — "Simond slides the Major's commission across the table towards Hercule. 'Er, sorry about the confusion, Sir Bonaparte!'"

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "*A pride filled Hercule, graciously accepts the commission and salutes his new commander.  He then takes his leave to go tell his love the good news.  He continues to slightly flourish his cane on the way out, not fully understanding why it was important, but not willing to argue against the practice*"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I will remain with my current Regiment as it were, as the position I was interested as been taken by Sir Bonaparte, who has proven himself in the field of battle.  Hercule I wish to congratulate you on this command - I'm sure it will be in good hands"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle - I wish to have words with you outside, cur"

### "I'll have an explanation, or I'll have blood"

*The words were had in the street, en passant:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "Curtis Sinnoch What can I do for you, sir?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle Twice now you have insulted me.  Once behind closed doors, but I could forgive you for that - you have a hard time seeing properly that which is not directly infront of you, a Lavelle trait, I'm aware"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Sir, no insult was intended, nor I believe any made...?" — "As to the letter, you have since corrected that grievous error of judgment, and shown yourself the wiser for it."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "This time, you directly take away a Majority from be, speaking of your 'Leadership' to command when you couldn't lead a wet mop out of a closet!  Wasnt you, who have repeatedly stated the value of family names, ties, and the old blood - yet you willingly throw your weight behind a newly knighted commoner?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "As we agree that your previous regimental affiliation was an error, whatever can you be concerned over?" — "Sir, are you sure you wish me to explain my reasoning here in the street?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "*Curtis slowly draws his Sabre*" — "I'll have an explanation, or I'll have blood"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Sir, none can deny that between the two of you, Sir Bonaparte has more of the leader in him, new blood or no. This is no disparagement of your talents -- you are a fine soldier and leader in your own right. But just as you say, Sir Bonaparte comes from less than nothing and has nevertheless fought bravely." — "I deny that you have any cause to be insulted. If you insist, well, we shall see what our fellows think."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle at no point did I ever question his ability in battle or as a man - what has irked me here more then anything else is your consistent claim and clamor of the purity of the Old Blood, only to directly toss said words into my face with your actions"

*Jules answered with cosmology:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "If our blood has value, it is by virtue of its closeness to the source of nobility, the King himself. For what good is our aristocratic system, if, like the stars, it revolves not around a central body, deriving its strength and structure thereby?" — "The Earth is the center of the universe, the King is the center of France. And Sir Bonaparte has proved himself worthy of the King's blessing. He is certainly worthy of mine."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Your words are like the mewling of a child - caught in the act of stealing from the kitchen and then hurriedly putting together a fabrication to explain their actions.  If any of this were true, why would you not have arranged a letter before hand instead of directly doing so to my face?  Your explanation holds water like a sieve."

### The vote and the challenge

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "(I am challenging, and cause is nebulous.  Other Gentlemen, please vote if you think I do or do not have cause)"

**Lucien Vaillancourt** *(asked by the Narrator whether his was a vote for valid cause)* — "As long as I can vote from the battlefield"

**Amand Baschet** — "The noblemanfeels insulted. Therefore, I believe he has just cause. For we cannot ignore the heart from which all our lifeblood flows. However, I hope that a single drop off blood will suffice to repair M. Sinoch's honor."

*Cause found, Curtis lodged the formal challenge in his customary style:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle - Stand and Deliver!  I have been practicing with my daggers!  Though... this is a disagreement and a demand for satisfaction.  I shall be using my Sabre"

*And while the seconds were arranging matters, the Sinnoch family settled its own account with the man who had invented the cane bonus:*

**Narrator** — "The Sinnochs have sent a messenger to the Minister of War's office pointing out the great service their family has offered the military in the past. The next day, Acting Commander Germain Simond is summarily dismissed and sent to the Frontier Regiments for his shameful mishandling of the application process."

**Narrator** — "Curtis has been given the rank of junior Major. The Minister of War attempts to have him appointed Acting Regimental Commander, but the King intervenes, insisting the commander of the Royal Foot Guards must bear a title in his own right." — "Hercule is now the Acting Commander of the Royal Foot Guards."

*So by the time the duelists met, both were Royal Foot Guards after all — junior Major Sinnoch and Captain Lavelle, under Acting Commander Sir Hercule Bonaparte, the very man the blood was to be shed over.*

## The duel

**Narrator** — "The gentlemen step out into the street, the entire regiment looking on. A duel that sheds noble blood is no laughing matter. Curtis Sinnoch's eyes blaze with righteous indignation. Jules Lavelle seems resigned, but not afraid."

**Narrator** — "Each man wields his sabre, not yet having adopted the regiment's own weapon as their preferred killing implement."

**Jules Lavelle** — "If I don't survive, ask Lady Landry to visit my grave every full moon."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "If you don't survive, it is your own doing - I have no intention on killing you, Jules Lavelle, merely to teach a lesson"

**Jules Lavelle** — "This duel is a lesson in your fragile ego, and nothing more. En Garde!"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Have at you!"

### Round One

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I REST"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I jump back!"

### Round Two

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I REST"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I rest"

### Round Three

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I SLASH"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I block!"

**Narrator** — "Curtis frowns in dismay as Jules anticipates his strike!"

*Curtis asked whether the blocked stroke might break a blade; the Narrator ruled no — "No breakage when weapons are the same."*

### Round Four

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I REST"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I parry"

### Round Five

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I REST"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I jump back"

### Round Six

**Jules Lavelle** — "I rest"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I SLASH"

**Narrator** — "Curtis continues his assault, dealing 30 damage to Jules. The Royal Foot Guards politely applaud."

**Jules Lavelle** — "Such brazen insolence."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "And with first blood, I know my fury was righteous!  I've claimed my blood, sir, and know it was just.  If you stand down, I shall follow suit"

**Jules Lavelle** — "So speaks the coward!"

### Round Seven

**Jules Lavelle** — "I rest"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I REST"

### Round Eight

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I REST"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I block"

### Round Nine

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I CUT"

**Jules Lavelle** — "I rest"

**Narrator** — "Jules fumbles with his blade, his blood-slicked hands unable to grip properly. Curtis seizes the advantage, cutting his thigh to the quick. Jules drops to one knee, barely supporting his weight, using his blade as a cane."

### Round Ten

**Jules Lavelle** — "I surrender"

## The issue

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "*Curtis wipes his blade clean, sheathes it, and mentions to the Foot Guard to assist Jules*"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I wish for no more blood of yours, sir - my thirst, and that of my blade, are both Slaked.  Where shall we take you to get patched up?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Away with you! To attack a regimental fellow over a difference in military strategy? I'll have no more of your company, sir."

**Narrator** — "Curtis gains one expertise and loses 1 status point for beating a friend."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I believe you and I have quite different definitions of 'military strategy' and 'outright insult' - I shall give you the peace you request.  Good Day."

**The reckoning:** Jules Lavelle — surrendered on Round Ten, opened by a 30-damage slash to which the Foot Guards politely applauded, then cut to the quick of the thigh and left kneeling on his own sabre as a cane; and, by the Narrator's image, a man who had used a cane to steal a Majority was now beaten down to using one. Curtis Sinnoch — untouched through all ten rounds, +1 Expertise, −1 status for shedding a regimental friend's blood, and a lesson delivered exactly as promised.

*The irony of the day belonged to Hercule. That same evening, the man the duel was fought over dined at Hunter's in triumph:*

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "*For the first time, Hercule enters Hunter's under the clout of his own name, his presence no longer requiring the grace of others.  Tonight's plans: a quiet meal at a table for two with the beauty Alida.  And while their date is clearly a private affair, none mistake that she has dressed to capture the eyes of the room*"

**Narrator** — "The maître d' scrambles over, waving at the sommelier. 'Sir Bonaparte and the lustrous Mlle. Demachy,' he says, bowing deeply. 'May Hunter's present this finest bottle of 1599 Bordeaux, with our compliments to the hero!'"

### The reconciliation

*The next day — Curtis having just reached the twelfth social level, the threshold of the most exclusive club in Paris — the victor came looking for his victim in the streets:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Jules Lavelle - No hard feelings, eh?  Having a day or two, I understand (though I don't agree) your reason for speaking up prior.  Sir Hercule does have quite the military aptitude and leadership ability.  I wish to make it up to you, if you aren't feeling too stiff.  Would you care to join me at Bothwell's this last week of the Month?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "*Jules's eyes light up with the prospect of entry to the Promised Land of gentleman's clubs. The fury in his heart evaporates in an instant.*" — "By all means, good sir!" — "I do hope they won't mind if I get blood on the upholstery. The thigh wound is a bit of a seeper."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Yes... Yes... *Curtis Glances away slightly* Sorry about that one... It was an open shot... perhaps I should have pulled the blade back a little on that swing..."

**Jules Lavelle** — "I worry to bring my lady along. What if she were to find Hunter's no longer sufficient?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Indeed - I'll be putting in my membership earlier in the month, but this will be my first visit proper.  Lets leave our fine women at home - get the feel of the club, and have a proper breaking in!"

*The visit came off that December, and the record of it is the whole of the peace treaty:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, a lovely Christmas, is it not!  To be spending at one of the finest establishments this fine City has to offer!" — "Jules Lavelle, good sir, look around - what do you see!"

**Narrator** — "Though nominally a club, Bothwell's has none of the customary trappings of such an establishment. Occupying a three-floor manse on the Seine just past the Wall of Charles V, members are each assigned a private room, replete with waitstaff, musicians, and the finest culinary output of French gastronomy and viniculture. The intricate Baroque woodwork on every wall speaks to the height of architectural fashion, but as loud as the decor may be, the watchword of Bothwell's is discretion. Members enter and exit at all hours, never crossing paths with one another except by their own deliberate choice and prearrangement. The King himself is said to frequent the club on occasion, yet still the luminaries of Paris make it their refuge on all other days."

**Jules Lavelle** — "My, what an experience. What is this drink they're serving? 'Chocolate?' Is it healthy?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Regardless, it sure is delicious!  I can certainly see the benefits of having it when the air chills!  What a glorious evening!  To our health!"

*Fifteen months after sabres in the street, the two would be back at Bothwell's together, Jules toasting Curtis's birthday at the no-limit gaming table. The friendship held for the rest of the game.*
