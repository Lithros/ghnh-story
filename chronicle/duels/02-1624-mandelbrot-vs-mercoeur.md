# Leon Mandelbrot vs. Etienne de Mercoeur

*March 1624 · the doorstep of Adélaïde Blaise, Paris · Cause: rival suitors for the same lady · rapier against rapier*

The second duel of the game, fought the day after Aveline and Beaufosse crossed blades over the Demachy twins — and the first true test of the dueling rules. Where the first affair ended in six turns on a shattered cutlass, this one ran nineteen turns of rapier-play, five wounds against three, and left both suitors standing in their own blood before the lady's window. It set the standard every later duel would be measured against, and it delivered the campaign's first great lesson in the cruelty of seventeenth-century romance: the lady wanted a corpse.

## The quarrel

**Narrator** — "Renowned far and wide for her beauty (and no less for her family's connections), Adélaïde Blaise has long been one of the most sought-after young women in Paris. Leon Mandelbrot had this in mind as he arrived at her doorstep to make clear his intentions and win her heart. But as he raised his hand to the knocker, he felt a tap at his shoulder. Etienne de Mercoeur stood behind, well-bred eyes narrowed at Leon's impertinence. Young Adélaïde peered out from the window, and the men knew they were at an impasse. A crowd began to form."

*The crowd knew its duty by now — this was the second such doorstep in two days:*

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "These men of status clearly seek the same heart! Let them enter formal combat!"

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "May their blades be as stout as their hearts."

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "Leon Mandelbrot I bear you no ill will for seeking the company of a lady so fair, but her magnificence implores that I challenge you for her hand. Draw, good sir."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "I accept your challenge Etienne de Mercoeur, and I complement your fine tastes!"

**Narrator** — "Name your weapons."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "/draws rapier"

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "A fine weapon, sir. /draws rapier"

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "Touche!"

**Narrator** — "Etienne has advantage."

*The Narrator repeated the standing rules of the list — players track their own wounds, a mandatory rest enters each sequence below half endurance, and at zero endurance you are dead — while the spectators settled in.*

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "These men appear to be quite evenly matched."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "/say You appear a worthy opponent!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "We shall see who has the first touch!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "These men seem like they are full of vigor- it may be a protracted bout!"

## The issue

*Nineteen turns. Etienne de Mercoeur took five wounds — 22, 11, 22, 11, and 11 — for a total of 77 endurance lost; Leon Mandelbrot took three thrusts of 18 in return, 54 in all. Neither man had surrendered an inch until Etienne's legs made the argument for him.*

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "No offense to the competitors from a couple weeks ago, but this was a true match."

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "Ye gods, I did not think I would encounter such a vigorous opponent"

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "Rapier against rapier, the consummate battle between gentlemen."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "I wipe the blood off my sword and sheathe with a flourish, before offering my hand to Etienne"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I do agree, dear Guillaume, though I may also take offense and reserve it for a later time!"

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "A most excellent battle , with a most excellent opponent!"

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "/takes the hand, wholeheartedly"

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "You were robbed of the chance to show your valor, good Godefroy!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "I am sure other chances will present themselves."

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "I doff my cap to you, and wish you luck with the good mademoiselle"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Pray you do not give him cause to now"

**Narrator** — "Lady Adélaïde throws wide her door!"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "Congratulations to the victor!"

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "My dearest Lady Adelaide! I have bled and drawn blood for your favor!"

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "/bows deeply, to hide the grimace of pain"

**Godefroy de Beaufosse** — "An exceedingly well-fought match by both."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Let us see if the lady reciprocates!"

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "Indeed sir, monsieur de Mercoeur was a most vigorous and excellent opponent!"

*Then came the gesture that made Etienne de Mercoeur's reputation as the campaign's first true gentleman — the loser, still bleeding from five wounds, extending hospitality to the man who gave them:*

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "My thanks, Godefroy. And to show that I bear no ill will, Leon, I invite you to be my companion at the Hunter's next month, once I have had time to heal from my wounds."

**Guillaume d'Avergny** — "My, a true gentleman. Noble blood shows, after all."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "Alas, I am not born of the nobility, but I aspire to reach the heights of the true essence of such"

*And then the lady rendered her verdict. In the courting channel, minutes after the door swung open:*

**Narrator** — "Leon Mandelbrot , roll to court Adélaïde (SL 9)."

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "Good fortune be with you!"

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "Lets go dice golem!"

> 🎲 Leon Mandelbrot rolls 1d6 — **1** (courting Adélaïde Blaise, SL 9: failure)

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "-_-"

**Narrator** — "Utterly disappointed at your failure to kill Etienne, she soundly rejects you."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "Madame, I am a gentleman, not a murder! To such, an honorable duel may carry more weight then your current rejection. But fear not, I will show you that my heart is true!"

**Jean-Luc Aveline** — "You are one of the luckiest men I know. Unfortunately, bad luck is luck."

*So the victor won nothing but his wounds — Guillaume's grim proverb about killing in a lady's name proved to be the house rule of Adélaïde Blaise's heart. No expertise award was posted on the channel for this bout, but the arithmetic tells its own story: Etienne held the advantage here, and when the same two men compared notes before their rematch in May, both stood equal at rapier expertise 11 — the victor's point duly banked. The month's ledger was crueler still: when status was reckoned at March's end, Leon reported his social level had fallen from 3 to 2, blood and glory notwithstanding.*

*The friendship, at least, was real. On the streets in the days that followed:*

**Alphonse Devereux** — "Leon Mandelbrot, thank you for your kind greeting! I've come to see Paris in the spring and the ladies similarly blooming. I hear you recently won a duel handily."

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "Leon Mandelbrot - Oh, la la. Have you seen the gazette this month? Seems we are rather well favored, you and I."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "So it would seem! We shall storm this country and make names for ourselves yet!"

*And the Hunter's invitation held. In April, Etienne — newly a captain of the Cardinal's Guard — was arranging the party from the club floor:*

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "Curtis Sinnoch Perhaps you would consider a small change this coming month? I will be hosting Mssrs Mandelbrot and Loafmann, and we would surely enjoy a fourth."

**Etienne de Mercoeur** — "We plan to meet the third week of May."

*They would need the fellowship. Before spring was out, Leon Mandelbrot and Etienne de Mercoeur found themselves at yet another lady's door — the fates, as Leon put it, truly against them — and the whole bloody business began again.*

**The reckoning:** Etienne de Mercoeur — yielded on turn nineteen, 77 endurance lost across five wounds. Leon Mandelbrot — victorious, 54 endurance lost across three wounds, rejected at the door by Adélaïde Blaise for failing to kill, and down a social level by month's end. Both men — a friendship sealed in blood and an engagement at the Hunter's.
