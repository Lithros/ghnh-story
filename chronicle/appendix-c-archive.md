# Appendix C — The Archive

*Documents recovered from the campaign's first year and its wars: the Gazette de France's surviving issues, the army's organizational charts, and the imagery the Narrator used to dress the front.*

## The Gazette de France (1624–1625)

The Cardinal's newspaper ran monthly through the game's first year, written entirely by the Narrator: social pages tracking every courtship, military news seeding every campaign, editorials "from the desk of the Cardinal," and a permanent subheader — *One injured, none dead* — that was never once accurate. It suspended publication during campaign seasons ("Lazy git." — a reader) and went on indefinite hiatus in 1626, when the Narrator's real-world firstborn arrived.

## A note on sources

This chronicle was compiled from the complete Discord export of the *Great Hearts, Noble Hearts* server: forty-two channels, roughly 12,800 messages, spanning August 2020 to September 2024 (game time March 1624 to early 1629). The channels divided into gameplay (campaign, duels, courting, chargen, military-off-season, cemetery), locales (five clubs, one bawdyhouse, and the streets of Paris), eighteen regimental messes, and the Gazette, announcements, and administrative channels. The game ran under the *En Garde!* rules (Hany & Chadwick, GDW 1975; UK 4th edition), with the Narrator's house rules for marriage, mistress retirement, court trials, government office, and — fatefully — thrown weapons. Events were reconstructed in game-time order by cross-referencing the announcements calendar against message timestamps; character successions were established from the dice bot's echoes of players' mourning nicknames, the character-generation channel, and the tombstones themselves. All quoted dialogue is verbatim from the record, lightly normalized for spelling.
