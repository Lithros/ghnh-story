# Spring 1625 — The Fall of Nantes

*March – May 1625 · resolved at the table 8 November 2020 · Mission: Defense · Theater: Nantes, on the Loire*

---

## The situation

The winter had ended in humiliation at Blavet, and the spring began with the bill coming due. When the mission die came up **5**, the Narrator declared it:

**Narrator** — "The Spring campaign is a Defense mission."

**Narrator** — "The Huguenots, fresh from their victory at Blavet, have raised a large army. Their navy has already set sail, and the army now travels south, seeking to link up with the larger part of the Huguenot force at La Rochelle."

**Narrator** — "The Frontier Regiments have been deployed to Nantes to set up a defensive line. Success here will slow or halt the Huguenot advance. But the Protestants outnumber the Catholics three to one. Their approaching flags seem to stretch forever."

*Only two gentlemen of the table stood in that line — the same two majors who had waded ashore at Blavet and lived. Curtis Sinnoch's farewell to the capital was, as ever, delivered at full voice:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, the feel of the wind!  The air itself!  It stirs me and my men to action!  Farewell, Paris, for @Curtis Sinnoch marches to war!"

---

*The battalion volunteer rolls had gone out back in February. Among the units drawn for the front, two were no accident of the dice:*

**Narrator** — "KM3 is the choice of @Curtis Sinnoch" · "CPC3 is the choice of @WillWorker"

**Narrator** — "@Curtis Sinnoch and @WillWorker have volunteered on campaign."

**Narrator** — "Each leads his battalion under the Frontier Regiment Colonel, who is Colonel Christian Solé this season."

| Character | Rank / post | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| **Curtis Sinnoch** | Major, commanding 3rd Battalion | King's Musketeers, under the Frontier Regiment |
| **Hercule Bonaparte** | Major, commanding 3rd Squadron | Crown Prince Cuirassiers, under the Frontier Regiment |

Above them: **Colonel Christian Solé** of the Frontier Regiment, whose one recorded vignette is the campaign's whole tragedy in miniature —

**Narrator** — "Colonel Solé, clear-eyed and hale, delivers a speech in which he declares that the enemy may be numerous, but quality is on the side of the French Army."

Opposite them: the entire Huguenot field army, three men to every one of the King's.

*A battle result of 5 — on a scale where low numbers are glory and high numbers are ruin — and the Narrator supplied the prose to match:*

**Narrator** — "Unfortunately, the valorous part of the French Army is back in Paris. Mid-speech, Solé notices the Huguenots have begun their charge. He spurs his horse...away from the battlefield, leaving his adjutant to give orders."

**Narrator** — "@Curtis Sinnoch and @WillWorker, roll to determine your battle results as your positions are overrun by hordes of screaming Protestants."

### The King's Musketeers hold the line

**Curtis Sinnoch (player)** — "(1d6, right?)"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **6** · **Narrator** — "Battle result: 2"

**Narrator** — "Curtis leads his men in a desperate counter action. It seems that the quality of the King's Musketeers really does shine through, as the attackers melt away when faced with the fury of their musket fire."

**Narrator** — "Curtis, your rolls are Death 9, Despatches 9, Promotion 7, Crowns 6"

*Six minutes later the Narrator checked his arithmetic and dimmed the glory by one notch:*

**Narrator** — "Correction. You barely hold off the attackers. Your actual result is a 3."

**Narrator** — "Death 11, Despatches 11, Promotion 9, Crowns 9"

### The Cuirassiers charge

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 1d6 — **5** · **Narrator** — "Battle Result: 2"

**Narrator** — "The Cuirassiers charge over the battlefield, running roughshod over the underequipped and unprepared Protestant militia."

**Narrator** — "Death 10, Despatches 10, Promotion 8, Crowns 6"

### The verdict

*Two majors had beaten their colonel's battle result by two and three points respectively — and it was not enough. When both men's personal fates had been rolled out (see The reckoning), the Narrator pronounced the day:*

**Narrator** — "Despite great showings of valor from volunteer majors, the bulk of the Frontier Regiments is unable to resist the unrelenting assault from the Huguenots. Nantes is overrun, and Colonel Solé executed for his poltroonery."

*The colonel who fled his own speech was thus the campaign's only recorded officer fatality — shot not by the enemy but by the King's justice.*

## The reckoning

### Curtis Sinnoch — Major, 3rd Battalion, King's Musketeers

*Against targets of Death 11, Despatches 11, Promotion 9, Crowns 9, the hero of the Blavet boathouse played it straight for once:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I will not be attempting any reckless bravery"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Rolling for Death!"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 2d6 — **5** (Death target 11: survives) · **Narrator** — "Alive!"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 2d6 — **7** (Mention target 11: fails) · **Narrator** — "Unknown!"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 2d6 — **8** (Promotion) · **Narrator** — "Promoted!"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 2d6 — **5** (Crowns target 9: fails)

**Narrator** — "There are no open positions. You have been Mentioned in Despatches. Roll 1d6"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "+2 SP for the next 3 months, then +1 for life" — "er" — "+4" — "No loot"

**Narrator** — "Also, Curtis, for outperforming your commanding officer in the field, you gain +1 MA permanently."

*And then the sentence every ambitious officer waits for:*

**Narrator** — "As this is your second time being mentioned in despatches, the King has caught wind of your talents."

**Narrator** — "You may roll 1d6 to receive a Title on a roll of 6. This can be modified by Influence over the King, if you can muster it."

**Narrator** — "Actually, due to your high SL, you only need a roll of 5."

**Curtis Sinnoch (player)** — "king needs a Class 9 favor, right?"

**Narrator** — "Yes"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "The King is about to sign the decree, but is distracted by his mistress. Maybe next time..."

### Hercule Bonaparte — Major, 3rd Squadron, Crown Prince Cuirassiers

*Against targets of Death 10, Despatches 10, Promotion 8, Crowns 6 — no reckless bravery this time, the memory of Blavet's surf perhaps still fresh:*

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 2d6 *(#death)* — **6** (Death target 10: survives)

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 2d6 *(#despatches)* — **7** (fails)

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 2d6 *(#promo)* — **7** (fails)

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 2d6 *(#crowns)* — **5** (fails)

*Four flat failures after the death roll — until the GM checked the modifiers:*

**Lithros (GM)** — "Sorry, should have been another -1 on Mentions/Promotion" — "So you actually do qualify for promotion!" — "As no positions are open, please roll a die for Despatches."

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "enjoy +1 SP/month for the rest of your life. also +1 MA"

**Narrator** — "The King is informed of your outstanding service to France."

**Narrator** — "Roll 1d6 to see if you receive a Title. You would need a 6."

> 🎲 Hercule Bonaparte rolls 1d6 — **6** — a natural six, met at the table with a row of 😮 and 📜

**Narrator** — "Hercule Bonaparte has received the title of Knight!"

**Narrator** — "+10 SP next month"

**Narrator** — "Your SL is instantly raised to 10."

**WillWorker (player)** — "That's SIR Herule Bonaparte"

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "Hercule faces his knighthood with the same steel-eyed stone face he has become known for.  As the King presents the new knight to the Court, the exceptionally observant note a slight upward curl at the corners of his mouth"

*Minutes after the roll, the Narrator inaugurated the campaign's #peerage channel — its very first post, Hercule's patent of knighthood, saluted with six rounds of applause. The mechanics of the miracle were clarified afterward: the title chance came not from loot but from beating the fleeing Colonel Solé's battle result by three full points — a 2 against a 5.*

### Honors, casualties, and the ledger

No player character so much as bled at Nantes — the butcher's bill fell on the rank and file of the Frontier Regiments and on **Colonel Christian Solé**, executed for his poltroonery. **Curtis Sinnoch** came home with his second Mention in Despatches (+4 SP for three months, then +1 for life), a permanent +1 MA for outperforming his commanding officer, and a title roll spoiled by the royal mistress. **Hercule Bonaparte** came home a **Knight** — Mentioned in Despatches (+1 SP per month for life), +1 MA, +10 SP, and his Social Level vaulted instantly to 10. The city itself was the campaign's casualty: Nantes belonged to the Huguenots, and the road to La Rochelle lay open.

*There was no pause to mourn. That same evening the Minister of War drew up the summer's lists:*

**Narrator** — "The Minister of War decides who to draw up for the Summer Campaign."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **2**

**Narrator** — "Everyone except the 4th Foot Brigade is going on campaign."

**Narrator** — "That means everyone, since Pain Loafmann is the only one without a regiment and he is being volunteered."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 for brigade assignments — **6**

**Narrator** — "Field Operations for everybody!"

**ArcticFox (player)** — "YAY!" — "WE ALL GET TO DIE!"

### Aftermath: Sir Bonaparte at Hunter's

*The new knight could not collect his +10 SP until he returned to Paris — "You'll get it the month you come back," the Narrator ruled — and so the knighthood's true coronation came months later, in September, at the door of Hunter's:*

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "For the first time, Hercule enters Hunter's under the clout of his own name, his presence no longer requiring the grace of others.  Tonight's plans: a quiet meal at a table for two with the beauty Alida.  And while their date is clearly a private affair, none mistake that she has dressed to capture the eyes of the room"

**Narrator** — "The maître d' scrambles over, waving at the sommelier. 'Sir Bonaparte and the lustrous Mlle. Demachy,' he says, bowing deeply. 'May Hunter's present this finest bottle of 1599 Bordeaux, with our compliments to the hero!'"
