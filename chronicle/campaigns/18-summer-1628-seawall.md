# Summer 1628 — Sixty Sail Against the Seawall

*June – August 1628 · resolved at the table 5 February 2022 (deployment fixed 15 December 2021) · Mission: Siege · Theater: the seawall of La Rochelle*

---

## The situation

The summer's shape was set months in advance. On 15 December the Narrator announced, "The Minister of War decides the composition of forces for the summer campaign," and rolled:

> 🎲 The Minister of War rolls 1d6 — **4**; 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "A single field army will be deployed, with every brigade except the Horse Guards and the 1st Brigade of Foot."

**Narrator** — "This will be a Siege campaign."

*On 5 February the books opened on the season itself:*

**Narrator** — "The Summer Campaign"

**Narrator** — "The siege of La Rochelle continues. The English navy, under the Admiral of the Fleet, the Earl of Lindsey, has assembled a flotilla of 29 warships and 31 merchantmen, ready to force their way past the sea wall and either break the siege, or evacuate the city entirely."

![Robert Bertie, 1st Earl of Lindsey — the portrait posted with the fleet's arrival](../images/campaign/campaign_09_800px-Robert_Bertie2C_1st_Earl_of_Lindsey2C_by_circle_of_Michiel_Jansz_van_Mierevelt.png)

**Narrator** — "It is the largest naval assault ever conducted against the French homeland. If the English are able to breach the seawall, a fortification devised by Cardinal Richelieu himself, they will have direct access to the city, and the rebellion will spread like a cancer. This is the last chance to contain the Protestant venom."

**Narrator** — "The Cardinal himself has taken to the field, and will command the army on this campaign. He has positioned his headquarters on the seawall itself, and has consecrated every stone as a fortress in defense of God and France."

![Cardinal Richelieu on the seawall of La Rochelle — Henri Motte's painting, posted with the Cardinal's arrival](../images/campaign/campaign_10_1024px-Siege_of_La_Rochelle_1881_Henri_Motte.png)

## The record

### Supreme command — Cardinal Richelieu

**Narrator** — "The Cardinal delivers his orders, arranging the army in a horseshoe pattern to catch any stray English in their net."

![The investment of La Rochelle — the period siege map posted with the Cardinal's orders](../images/campaign/campaign_11_953315-1579612395.png)

> 🎲 The Cardinal's command roll: 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "For all his fervor, the Cardinal has little military background. The English admiral, noting the broad array facing his forces, opts to concentrate his push on one section of the seawall, wreaking tremendous damage. The result is 5 - Broken by sortie."

### 1st Division — Lt. Gen. Bernard Lefebvre

**Narrator** — "Lt. Gen. Lefebvre believes he understands the English mind well. He instructs his elite units to put their sharpshooting to the test, to try to pick off English officers aboard the attacking ships."

> 🎲 Lt. Gen. Lefebvre's command roll: 1d6 — **2**

**Narrator** — "His plan fails; skilled as the men are, the rolling waves, and huddled-together English soldiery, makes it nearly impossible to distinguish common man from officer. Still, the bullets do find home in English flesh. The result is 4 - Inconclusive."

### Guards Brigade — Bg. Gen. Alphonse Joseph Leclerc

**Narrator** — "Bg. Gen. Leclerc, realizing the full might of the English command staff will remain to direct the attack, orders his section of the seawall abandoned. His adjutant stares like Leclerc has gone mad, but he hushes the young officer and continues with the orderly evacuation."

> 🎲 Bg. Gen. Leclerc's command roll: 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "As the English ships collide with the empty section of the seawall, disgorging their howling passengers, the planned charge across the fortification is halted by the realization that the mortar for this part of the seawall has yet to fully settle! The English charge back onto their boats before the weight of the landing causes them to settle entirely. The attack is partly slowed, but not stopped. The result is 3 - Inconclusive."

### The Royal Foot Guards — Lt. Col. Jules Lavelle

**Narrator** — "Lt. Col. @Jules Lavelle, give your orders."

**Jules Lavelle** — "Appalled at the evident cowardice displayed by his commander, Jules orders his men to charge after the English, hoping to board and seize one of the merchantmen. 'For the Glory of Lavelle!'"

*Lavelle's command dice had come up six two campaigns running — a six in the Norman bocage in winter, a six on the beaches in spring. The streak chose this moment, at the rail of an English warship, to end.*

> 🎲 Lt. Col. Lavelle's command roll: 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "Jules manages to lead his men up onto the deck of a warship instead, where they are met by the astonishment, and professional savagery, of seasoned English sea dogs. The Royal Foot Guards are butchered, sent into the sea by flight or in pieces. The result is 6 - Scattered by sortie."

*A single musket emoji rose over the message in salute.*

### The King's Musketeers — Lt. Col. Adhemar Lucy

**Narrator** — "Lt. Col. Adhemar Lucy directs the King's Musketeers to follow the general's orders, maintaining the sniper fire even under retreat."

> 🎲 Lt. Col. Lucy's command roll: 1d6 — **5**

**Narrator** — "The shots are no more damaging than before, though no great harm befalls his men. The result is 3 - Inconclusive."

### The Cavalry Division — Lt. Gen. Denis Nee

**Narrator** — "As the 2nd Division maintains the rest of the seawall, the Cavalry Division finds itself milling about on land. Lt. Gen. Denis Nee has been ordered to 'use his discretion' in choosing where and how to meet the enemy. With little idea how the English might attack, he has chosen to arrange the Heavy Brigade on the northern shore, and the Dragoons Brigade on the southern. If the English attempt a landing, his men will be ready."

> 🎲 Lt. Gen. Nee's command roll: 1d6 — **2**

**Narrator** — "Unfortunately, while his men are ready, Nee is not -- he is still arranging the Dragoons to the south when half of the English fleet redirects its efforts from the failing seawall to make a landing on the northern shore. The result is 5 - Broken by sortie."

### The Heavy Brigade — Bg. Gen. Jean-Martin Desjardins

**Narrator** — "As the English disembark, Bg. Gen. Desjardins frantically considers how he can possibly repel an infantry charge with a couple regiments of horse. He dimly recalls an old military text that recommended something called gorilla tactics. He doesn't know quite what it means, but orders his men to ride into the woods, which he imagines is where a gorilla would prefer to fight."

> 🎲 Bg. Gen. Desjardins's command roll: 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "Over half the English follow the Heavy Brigade away from the shore, their strategic concerns overtaken by bloodlust. Unable to catch up with the horse, they accomplish little. It is not a glorious victory, but it is a real one for the Heavy Brigade. The result is 3 - Inconclusive."

### 1st Squadron, Crown Prince Cuirassiers — Lt. Col. Jean Renault

**Narrator** — "@Jean Renault, give your orders and roll."

**Jean Renault** — "we will flank the English as they chase Desjardins"

**Jean Renault** — "Avoiding the charge we ride to battle men!"

> 🎲 Lt. Col. Renault's command roll: 1d6 — **6**

**Narrator** — "The Crown Prince Cuirassiers attempt to break away from the rest of the brigade and circle back to find the English, but before they can form lines and return to give battle, the English have already given up and returned to their boats. The result is 3 - Inconclusive."

### The close of the battle

**Narrator** — "The rest of the battle proceeds similarly -- having gotten the lay of the land, the English then pull back without taking serious losses. The siege continues, but the core of the English fleet remains a threat, and retreats to the open sea to regroup. The Cardinal declares the day an enormous success. As the seawall is hastily reinforced, cries of despair can be heard from within the city proper. The Huguenots know their days are numbered."

### Jean Renault — Lieutenant Colonel, 1st Squadron, Crown Prince Cuirassiers

**Narrator** — "@Jean Renault, your rolls are Death 14, Mention 11, Promotion 9, Crowns 9"

**Jean Renault** — "Reckless bravery 1"

> 🎲 Jean Renault rolls 2d6 — **10** (Death target 14, less 1 for Reckless Bravery: survives)

**Jean Renault** — "For mention!"

> 🎲 Jean Renault rolls 2d6 — **8** (Mention target 11: no mention)

**Jean Renault** — "For promotion!"

> 🎲 Jean Renault rolls 2d6 — **7** (Promotion target 9: no promotion)

**Jean Renault** — "For $$$"

> 🎲 Jean Renault rolls 2d6 — **7** (Crowns target 9: no loot)

**Narrator** — "An uneventful outcome."

**Jean Renault** — "Survival is never uneventful!"

### Jules Lavelle — Baron, Lieutenant Colonel, Royal Foot Guards

*His battalion scattered into the sea, Lavelle faced the dice with no chance of plunder:*

**Narrator** — "@Jules Lavelle, your rolls are Death 12, Mention 8, Promotion 7. No chance for crowns."

> 🎲 Jules Lavelle rolls 2d6 death — **5** (Death target 12: survives)
> 🎲 Jules Lavelle rolls 2d6 mention — **3** (Mention target 8: no mention)
> 🎲 Jules Lavelle rolls 2d6 promotion — **5** (Promotion target 7: no promotion)

**Narrator** — "An uneventful outcome for the scion of Lavelle."

### Slade Imbee — Subaltern, F Company, King's Musketeers

**Narrator** — "@Slade Imbee, your rolls are Death 14, Mention 11, Promotion 10, Crowns 9"

> 🎲 Slade Imbee rolls 2d6 death — **8** (Death target 14: survives)
> 🎲 Slade Imbee rolls 2d6 mention — **7** (Mention target 11: no mention)
> 🎲 Slade Imbee rolls 2d6 promotion — **9** (Promotion target 10: no promotion)
> 🎲 Slade Imbee rolls 2d6 crowns — **10** (Crowns target 9: loot!)

**Narrator** — "Crowns received!"

**Narrator** — "Roll 2d6"

> 🎲 Slade Imbee rolls 2d6 crowns — **4**

*The award came with the season's most singular honor, narrated from the GM's own hand:*

**Narrator** — "After the battle, as you are nursing a musket wound, the Cardinal notices you. He shakes your hand, his eyes steely, and continues down the line of injured mine. As you are gathering up your awe, his attendant drops a purse of 400 crowns into your hand. 'Remember his Eminence's generosity, and our great victory,' he hisses, and follows after his master."

**Slade Imbee** — "Slade is honored and dumbfounded. 'feared the worst.' He thinks. 'Our near shameful marksmanship failed to deprive the English of their commanders as planned. Though we were victorious, it was not the rout that was planned for this day. I thought we would be punished. The Lord surely smiles on us and graces the Cardinal and his Eminence with generosity indeed!'.*

Smiling ear-to-ear he turns to move on to his next duty with exuberance when a twinge in his thigh halts him. 'Oh right... perhaps a medic for first to bandage this up'"

### Honors and casualties

*No player character died on the seawall, and none was mentioned or promoted; the season's whole ledger was Slade Imbee's 400 crowns and a handshake from Cardinal Richelieu. The NPC butcher's bill took longer to tally. In the Crown Prince Cuirassiers' regimental channel, minutes after his own rolls:*

**Jean Renault** — "So did any colonels die?"

**Narrator** *(from the GM's player account)* — "Haven't resolved the NPCs yet"

*Four days later, out of character:* **Narrator** — "Regiment rosters have been updated with deceased NPCs."

*One more small casualty of the summer, in the off-season channel while the guns still sounded — Côme Touchard's bid for a marine's coat:*

**Narrator** — "@Ghôst Touchard, roll to join the Royal Marines. You need a 5."

> 🎲 Côme Touchard rolls 1d6 — **1** (rejected; the message drew a lone 🚫)
