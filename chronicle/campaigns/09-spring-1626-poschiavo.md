# Spring 1626 — One Small Iron Lock: The Siege of Poschiavo

*April–May 1626 · resolved at the table 25–26 January 2021 · Mission: Siege · Theater: the Valtellina — the fortified town of Poschiavo, in the Alps*

---

## The situation

A year and a half after the gentlemen first bled in the Alpine valley (see [Fall 1624](03-fall-1624-valtellina.md)), the war for the Valtellina resumed. On the night of 25 January the Narrator opened **The Spring Campaign** with the fullest statement of the war's stakes the game ever received:

**Narrator** — "For many years France has worked to keep separate the many branches of the sprawling Habsburg dynasty. The insidious family's control of Spain and the Holy Roman Empire holds France in a vise, and only Cardinal Richelieu's deft maneuvering has kept its jaws from shutting closed. The Habsburgs seek to unite their empire across the Alps, and the corridor for this joining runs through the valley of Valtelline."

**Narrator** — "Although the people of Valtellina themselves are Protestant, the Swiss Grison League has aligned itself with France to liberate the region from the hands of the Spanish and Papal armies occupying the valley. With Venice and Savoy also providing support, this is France's best chance to drive a wedge between the Habsburgs and maintain its strategic position in Europe -- a cause worth cooperating with a few Protestants over."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 for the mission — **1**

**Narrator** — "This is a Siege campaign. The town of Poschiavo has been fortified and must be taken. Whoever controls Poschiavo controls Valtellina."

*The expedition itself had been raised weeks earlier, in the April rain, by volunteers. Major Marius Thibodeaux of the Royal Marines had been planning it since the winter thaw:*

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "@Jean Rochant what say you to fighting for our country in May? There's too much rain in April to do much but loaf about and drink. By May we should be invigorated and our hearts will be overflowing with wine and patriotism!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Look at this guy seeking advice from a...subaltern??? Such bonhomie in the Royal Marines; can't be good for morale."

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "This man is my senior in that he has battle experience with this regiment, I am still working my way through the ranks (admittedly at a frenzied pace). I am only just getting the measure of Paris, and I respect the interests of my men." — "Also, having just started a courtship myself with the lovely Angélique Beauchamp (and hearing sordid tales of the last time Jean was deployed), I wanted to allow him to have a month's warning in order to fully satisfy any curiosities his mistress might have in his absence"

*A week later the papers were signed:*

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "The Royal Marines intend to serve our great nation in May!" — "Then yep, I volunteer the 1st Battalion"

**Jules Lavelle** — "@Hercule Bonaparte, the Royal Foot Guards' 2nd Battalion will be deploying, whether or not the regiment goes, though I do hope to serve once more under your inspiring leadership."

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "@Jules Lavelle Of course the regiment shall be marching in full. I wouldn't have it any other way. We can have our mettle getting rusty, can we?"

### Honors and the ledger

Not one Frenchman of note died before Poschiavo, and the loot rained down in a way no campaign of the game had yet matched: **Jean Rochant**, an unmentioned subaltern, walked off with **1,800 crowns** — the biggest single haul — followed by **Jules Lavelle** (mentioned in despatches, +3 SP for three months, and **1,100 crowns** in gems prised from the town's coat of arms), **Hercule Bonaparte** (**900 crowns** and a resolution to do better), **Curtis Sinnoch** (his sixth mention, +5 SP, **800 crowns**, and a **Knighthood** from the King — worth 10 extra status points that month for his great prestige), and **Marius Thibodeaux**, first man into the town, who took **700 crowns** of the Papal treasure and **+1 MA** for outperforming his commander, a distinction he shared with Sir Curtis. *(The Narrator's last act of the night was a quiet unexplained bookkeeping roll — 2d6 "#RMcapA," a 2 — and nothing more was said of it.)*

The money did not stay idle. The next morning — **Narrator**: "The Minister of War, satisfied with the siege of Valtellina, prepares the Summer Campaign." — the victors of Poschiavo went shopping. **Jean Rochant** ("I may as well spend some of these riches before I die") bought a captaincy of E Company and then a majority on top of it, mounting himself on a horse named "Dice Daemon." **Marius Thibodeaux**, freshly SL 6, bought the open Lieutenant-Colonelcy for 140 crowns — "A bargain!" — making him, per the GM, acting regimental commander of the Royal Marines.
