# The Campaigns — a primary-source edition

*The twenty campaign seasons of the war, 1624–1629, as they were narrated at the table — the Narrator's battle vignettes, the command rolls, every player character's personal reckoning, and the table talk, preserved verbatim. These are the primary sources behind [the chronicle](../README.md); read them where the chronicle's summary leaves you wanting the whole night.*

### [Spring 1624 — First Blood on the Croquant Frontier](01-spring-1624.md)
*April–May 1624*  
Two volunteers march against the Croquant rebels: Pierre de Tête rides his squadron out of the collapsing frontier works intact, while Guillaume d'Avergny — the Narrator's own character — becomes the game's first grave.
**Outcome:** Crushing defeat: the frontier works fall to the rebels Donat and Barran; Guillaume d'Avergny is annihilated in the front line; Pierre de Tête alone withdraws his 3rd Squadron to safety, undecorated.

### [Summer 1624 — The Great Campaign against the Croquants](02-summer-1624.md)
*June–August 1624*  
General Barbier's drunken frontal assault feeds four heroes to the salt-tax rebels in a single night — Aveline, Beaufosse, Mercoeur, and Kaneda (crushed by a plank, as the old gypsy woman predicted) — before the pincer closes and the rebellion is wiped from the earth.
**Outcome:** Pyrrhic French victory: the rebel army annihilated by pincer and Donat and Barran hanged, but Jean-Luc Aveline, Godefroy de Beaufosse, Etienne de Mercoeur, and Phillipe du Kaneda all died; Pierre de Tête mentioned in despatches with 50 crowns plunder; Leon Mandelbrot promoted Captain.

### [Fall 1624 — "My Life for France!": The Siege of the Valtellina Forts](03-fall-1624-valtellina.md)
*September–November 1624*  
Col. Aymard hurls the Frontier Regiment at the gates while the Queen's Own Carabiniers' grapnels find no purchase — Curtis goes triple-reckless for nothing, and Alphonse pries a 200-crown coat-of-arms off the wall on the way out.
**Outcome:** Both assaults Inconclusive (the fort later fell when the defenders ran out of food); no PC deaths — Alphonse Devereux Mentioned in Despatches with 200 crowns of plunder, Curtis and Guy came home empty-handed, one Q.O.C. captain killed.

### [Winter 1624–25 — The Storming of Blavet](04-winter-1624-25-blavet.md)
*December 1624 – February 1625*  
Nevers breaks the Huguenot fleet, Vendôme bungles the landing, Leon Mandelbrot drowns under his own prophesied plank, Guy de Cavoye explodes amid lacrosse-stick grenades — and Curtis Sinnoch walks off with a 1,000-crown boathouse chest as France withdraws.
**Outcome:** French defeat — the fort holds and Soubise names himself "Admiral of the Protestant Church"; Leon Mandelbrot and Guy de Cavoye die; Henri Toulouse is promoted Captain; Curtis Sinnoch and Hercule Bonaparte are mentioned in despatches.

### [Spring 1625 — The Fall of Nantes](05-spring-1625-nantes.md)
*Spring 1625 (March–May 1625)*  
Colonel Solé gallops away from his own battle speech and is shot for it, while the two majors who held the line come home with a second Mention and a natural-6 knighthood — but Nantes burns behind them.
**Outcome:** Defeat — Nantes overrun by the Huguenots; Col. Solé executed for poltroonery; Curtis Sinnoch earns his 2nd Mention but fumbles his title roll; Hercule Bonaparte knighted on a natural 6 (SL 10)

### [Summer 1625 — Slaughter in the Woods of Nantes](06-summer-1625-forest.md)
*June–August 1625*  
The Guards Brigade guns down its own Frontier Division in the forest, a colonel screaming "FOR FRANCE!" kills Pierre de Tête, and the catastrophe births the French Protestant Republic on August 8th.
**Outcome:** Royal disaster — Pierre de Tête killed; convict Pain Loafmann and Curtis Sinnoch mentioned in despatches; Alphonse Devereux promoted Major; La Rochelle joins Soubise and the French Protestant Republic is proclaimed.

### [Fall 1625 — Île de Ré: Not a Single (Enemy-Inflicted) Casualty](07-fall-1625-ile-de-re.md)
*September – November 1625*  
Soubise's island fortress falls in a flawless assault that costs France nothing — except Lucien Vaillancourt to a rotten plank, Pain Loafmann to his own sentence, and three NPC officers to the Duke's poisoned wine.
**Outcome:** Decisive royal victory: fortress stormed, Duke of Soubise captured; Lucien Vaillancourt and Pain Loafmann die; Clemens Charron promoted to Captain and loots Soubise's 1400-crown tea service; Bonaparte brevetted Colonel; Rochant promoted Subaltern; Sinnoch mentioned.

### [Winter 1625–26 — No Quarter on the Sèvre Niortaise](08-winter-1625-26-sevre.md)
*December 1625 – February 1626*  
Hercule's Royal Foot Guards ambush traitor militia at the river ferries with orders of no quarter, reaping a cascade of promotions — while Alphonse Devereux goes out in a blaze of glory, musketball between the eyes.
**Outcome:** Decisive victory (battle result 2): Hercule promoted to full Colonel, Curtis to Lieutenant Colonel, Jules to brevet Major; Alphonse Devereux killed in his chosen blaze of glory.

### [Spring 1626 — One Small Iron Lock: The Siege of Poschiavo](09-spring-1626-poschiavo.md)
*April–May 1626*  
The alliance forgets its siege equipment at Poschiavo, so Marius smashes the gate lock with a cutlass hilt, Curtis's snap volley torches the wall — and his sixth mention buys him a knighthood while loot rains on everyone.
**Outcome:** Victory — Poschiavo taken; no player deaths; Curtis Sinnoch knighted on his 6th mention; 5,300 crowns of plunder shared (Jean Rochant's 1,800 the biggest haul); Marius and Curtis each gain 1 MA for outperforming their commanders.

### [Summer 1626 — Rennes: "His Moustaches Are Rather Inspiring"](10-summer-1626-rennes.md)
*June–August 1626*  
Richelieu sends a task force to drag the Chalais conspirators out of Rennes, and France's finest foot commander charges through the gates alone — once.
**Outcome:** Rennes falls; Bg. Gen. Vasseur bayoneted alone in the gates; Marius Thibodeaux made Colonel of the Royal Marines; Guerinet Durand mentioned and 1,400 crowns richer; no player deaths.

### [Fall 1626 — FOR SOUBISE! The Ambush in the Cabbage Fields of Andorra](11-fall-1626-andorra.md)
*October–November 1626*  
Richelieu's secret peace mission is ambushed by English-armed "farmers" screaming FOR SOUBISE! — the ambush is annihilated, but Jean Rochant bleeds out with his cutlass buried in one last foe.
**Outcome:** French victory — the Cardinal saved and the English-backed ambush wiped out; Jean Rochant dies; Jules Lavelle knighted; Hercule Bonaparte made brevet Brigadier-General.

### [Winter 1626–27 — Jersey: "The Oily Surface Ignites"](12-winter-1626-27-jersey.md)
*December 1626 – February 1627*  
France's unofficial reprisal raid on Jersey razes Saint-Hélier, but a cackling old man with a lantern turns the fish-oiled harbor into an inferno that claims the Royal Marines.
**Outcome:** Saint-Hélier razed; Jaques Peugeot dies through a burning dock plank and Clemens Charron takes a hunting arrow through the heart; Marius Thibodeaux and Hercule Bonaparte make Brigadier-General; Curtis Sinnoch's paperwork-error barony becomes the RFG colonelcy; the Cardinal's Guard vanishes to a man.

### [Spring 1627 — Chevreuse: The Campaign of Three Graves](13-spring-1627-chevreuse.md)
*March–May 1627*  
France wins the assault on Chevreuse, but a twirling two-by-four, a hooded pistol shot from the Duke of Soubise, and a shaving razor fill three graves in one victorious afternoon.
**Outcome:** Victory for France — town burned, castle surrendered — but Pépin Bonheur, Bg. Gen. Hercule Bonaparte, and Cpt. Henri Toulouse all died; Soubise escaped.

### [Summer 1627 — The Flaming Galleon of Île de Ré](14-summer-1627-ile-de-re.md)
*June – August 1627*  
Buckingham lands on the Île de Ré, a burning galleon bears down on the French beachhead, and Jean Renault laughs off a joust to loot the cross of Lancelot du Lac.
**Outcome:** French victory — Buckingham's field army shattered, zero player deaths; Marius knighted, Curtis made Baron (then Marquis), Robert d'Engaux promoted.

### [Fall 1627 — The Battle of the Scarecrows](15-fall-1627-la-rochelle.md)
*September – November 1627*  
La Rochelle opens its gates to Buckingham, the Marines catch the Duke mid-escape, and a battalion bayonets a line of scarecrows while grouse and caltrops claim two heroes of France.
**Outcome:** Royal victory: Buckingham captured, Jules Lavelle made Baron; Sébastien Lefebvre torn apart by grouse, Robert d'Engaux dies refusing capture, Primo Tremblay found hiding inside a scarecrow.

### [Winter 1627–28 — The Baronets' Raid on the King](16-winter-1627-28-baronets.md)
*December 1627 – February 1628*  
English baronets swarm the Norman bocage to seize King Louis; Col. Kaplan's spittle-soaked charge kills Jerome Greybeck in a hedgerow, Primo Tremblay is dragged to death behind his own horse, and Jean Renault rides out of the wreckage a lieutenant colonel.
**Outcome:** French victory — the King saved and the baronets driven back to the coast; Jerome Greybeck and Primo Tremblay killed; Jean Renault mentioned and promoted to full Lieutenant Colonel.

### [Spring 1628 — The Slaughter on the Beaches](17-spring-1628-beaches.md)
*March – May 1628*  
Sea dogs and surf-born Reavers turn the La Rochelle beaches red — four player characters die in one afternoon, one under a falling galleon, one erased by temporal paradox, and two by the same cannonball.
**Outcome:** French victory on the field, but the deadliest resolution of the war: Marius Thibodeaux, Jean Shawinigan, Anton Dechets, and Pierre d'Amboise all killed; the Frontier Regiment shattered; Curtis Sinnoch takes the dead man's vacancy as Brevet Brigadier-General and Slade Imbee is promoted subaltern.

### [Summer 1628 — Sixty Sail Against the Seawall](18-summer-1628-seawall.md)
*June – August 1628*  
The Earl of Lindsey hurls sixty English sail at Richelieu's consecrated seawall, Jules Lavelle's string of sixes dies on the deck of a warship, and a wounded Slade Imbee gets a handshake from the Cardinal himself.
**Outcome:** The seawall holds and the English fleet retreats to regroup; no player character dies — Lavelle's Foot Guards are butchered boarding a warship but he survives unhonored, and Slade Imbee takes a musket wound, 400 crowns, and the Cardinal's handshake.

### [Fall 1628 — The Fall of La Rochelle, and a Vortex of Leafy Death](19-fall-1628-fall-of-la-rochelle.md)
*September – November 1628*  
La Rochelle falls and the army is set to checkpoint duty — where a lumpy cabbage cart erupts into a vortex of leafy death, and Côme Touchard survives the ambush only to die of a grieving greengrocer's bite.
**Outcome:** Every unit driven from the field by cabbages, spies, and rioting refugees; Côme Touchard dies of gangrene ("MY CABBAGESSSSSSS!"); Curtis Sinnoch made permanent Brigadier-General; Dufour and Blastagarde get acting battalion commands; the Cardinal declares the European theater at an end.

### [Winter 1629 — The Ambush on the Creuse (the Last Campaign)](20-winter-1629-creuse.md)
*February–March 1629*  
Every player volunteers for one last winter campaign to trap the Duke of Rohan on the river Creuse — Le Vaporisateur fires once and falls on its inventor, Rohan personally takes Armand Dufour's arm, and Slade Imbee wins a duel no one will ever know about.
**Outcome:** Pyrrhic French victory; Rohan escapes south. Albert Blastagarde, Armand Dufour, and Slade Imbee die; Curtis Sinnoch is created Earl and Brevet Lieutenant General; Jules Lavelle is made Colonel; Jean Renault inherits his squadron's 1,700-crown tontine and calls the final toast at the Horse Guards.
