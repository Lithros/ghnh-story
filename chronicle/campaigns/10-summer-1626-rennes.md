# Summer 1626 — Rennes: "His Moustaches Are Rather Inspiring"

*June – August 1626 · resolved at the table 22–23 February 2021 · Mission: Siege · Theater: Rennes, Brittany*

---

## The situation

This was the summer the war turned inward. No Habsburgs, no Huguenot republic — the enemy this season was a fortified French city sheltering French conspirators from a French cardinal. The Narrator set the board just before midnight on 22 February:

**Narrator** — "The Comte de Chalais, Henri de Talleyrand-Périgord, stands accused by Cardinal Richelieu of conspiring to assassinate His Eminence. As the investigation began, the Comte fled to the fortress of the Governor of Brittany and Duke of Vendôme, César de Bourbon, the king's own half-brother."

**Narrator** — "Cardinal Richelieu has asked the Minister of War to send a small task force to retrieve these and several other conspirators from Rennes, where they've gone into hiding. Brittany's governor is not without some military might of his own, and as the contingent of Royal troops approaches the city, it becomes clear that they are approaching a fortified target."

*The call-up had come weeks earlier in the off-season channel, on the heels of the spring victory in the Valtellina:*

**Narrator** — "The Minister of War, satisfied with the siege of Valtellina, prepares the Summer Campaign."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "The following brigades will be called up: 1st Foot Brigade, 3rd Foot Brigade, 4th Foot Brigade, Frontier Regiments, RNHB Regiment"

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "this will be a Siege campaign." *(Players affected by call: Marius Thibodeaux, Jean Rochant, Guerinet Durand.)*

*The Marines went shopping before they went to war. Marius bought the open Lieutenant-Colonelcy of the Royal Marines for 140 crowns — "A bargain!" — which made him acting regimental commander; Jean Rochant bought the majority that fell vacant beneath him, "120 crowns + 200 more for horses." His new horse was named "Dice Daemon." From the interim months at the front, one dispatch survives:*

**Jean Rochant** — "Meanwhile Mjr. Rochant is leading his troops, helping them avoid planks and cannonballs in the field of battle"

*Not everyone got to go. Col. Hercule Bonaparte volunteered the Royal Foot Guards for the summer and was overruled by the Minister of War himself, in a letter that caught his carriage just before it left Paris:*

**Narrator** — "Col. Bonaparte, It buoys my heart to know of the resolve and zeal with which you would commit your men to the defense of France. However, France is large, and I have already apportioned the appropriate units for the summer campaign. I have determined that your unit is needed to maintain the defense of Paris; as you know, the security of the capital is our chief concern in the face of heavy Habsburg maneuvering. When the danger is less, you are welcome to volunteer your men freely in another season. But for this summer, at least, you are staying put. With resolve, Minister Rouvier"

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "Hercule turns his carriage around and returns home to spend the month planning a new campaign with his beau: if the Minister of War refuses his brilliance, then the only solution is to become the Minister of War." *(four 😮 reactions)*

*In the announcements channel, the month of the assault reads, in full: "August 1626 — The month passes uneventfully in Paris."*

## The record

**Narrator** — "General Masse arrays his forces. Rennes is small, but walled, and cannon have been allocated at key locations, forming a wall of fire to repel all who might seek to attack the main gate."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "The General rides up and down the lines, shouting his plan for the first division to engage in a three-pronged assault. Nobody can quite hear or understand him, but his moustaches are rather inspiring. The result is 3, Inconclusive."

**Narrator** — "Lt. General Auclair nods his understanding, and directs his brigades to move forwards."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "The brigadier generals accept the straightforward order without question. The result is 3, Inconclusive."

**Narrator** — "Bg. General Vasseur, eager for the 1st Brigade to distinguish itself, orders a bit greater speed of march. The 1st Brigade has the honor of taking the main road into town, and needs not concern itself with navigating uneven terrain, but has little cover to show for it."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **2**

**Narrator** — "As the cannons open fire, the men of the Royal Marines and Picardy Musketeers show their mettle, and refuse to break. The result is 4, Inconclusive."

*Then the Narrator turned to the acting commander of the Royal Marines:*

**Narrator** — "Marius, you have been ordered to advance on the gate in the face of withering cannon fire. Roll for your result."

> 🎲 Marius Thibodeaux rolls 1d6 — **1** *(😮 😬)*

**Narrator** — "Your men quail, but the iron discipline of the Marines shines through. You only need to slap a few men with the back of your cutlass to encourage them forward, even as their comrades are blown to pieces around them. The result is 4, Inconclusive."

**Narrator** — "Col. Côté of the Picardy Musketeers, seeing an opportunity, rushes his regiment forward."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **6**

**Narrator** — "Screaming and firing their muskets, the Musketeers successfully drive the defenders from the walls, leaving the city gate defenseless. The result is a 2, Success by storming party." *(👏 👏)*

*And then the finest foot commander the French Army has to offer spent his reputation in a single stroke:*

**Narrator** — "The Musketeers tear the gates open. Desirous of glory, Vasseur charges his horse, first inside the walls. He finds himself alone and encircled by the terrified defenders. They pull the Bg. General from his horse and bayonet him without mercy as the horrified Musketeers try to rush to his aid."

**Narrator** — "Marc Ducournau, Captain of the Royal Marines C Company, is one of the unlucky few struck by a rampaging cannonball. He does not survive his injuries."

*The city's fate was pronounced the next afternoon, after the last personal rolls were in:*

**Narrator** — "An uninspired army has overwhelmed an underwhelming defense. The city of Rennes has fallen. The conspirators are quickly clapped in irons, along with a few of their servants for good measure, and the lot are shipped off to Nantes to stand trial."

### Honors and the ledger

Rennes fell, the Chalais conspirators went to Nantes in irons, and every player character marched home. **Marius Thibodeaux** came back a **brevet Colonel of the Royal Marines** — commanding the regiment he had bought his way into as a captain only eight game-months before. **Guerinet Durand** was **Mentioned in Despatches** (+5 SP for three months, +1 per month thereafter) and carried off **1,400 crowns**, the lion's share of the plunder for being first through the gate without being bayoneted for it. **Jean Rochant** rolled snake eyes against Death and precisely nothing else — no mention, no promotion, no crowns — but honored the reverse suicide pact and lived. The butcher's bill was borne by the NPCs: **Bg. General Robert Vasseur**, finest foot commander in the French Army, pulled from his horse and bayoneted alone inside the gates; and **Capt. Marc Ducournau** of the Royal Marines C Company, killed by a rampaging cannonball.

*The morning after the resolution, Maj. Ladagnous volunteered the Picardy Musketeers 2nd Battalion — Guerinet included — straight into the Fall campaign:*

**Jean Rochant** — "@DancingHorse Right back into the fray then?"

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "I imagine you'll have a few sleepless nights on the front in the coming days"

**Guerinet Durand** — "Just when I was planning to use my newfound influence and wealth with the ladies!!"

*And in the streets of Paris, the Marines came home:*

**Jean Rochant** — "Marius Thibodeaux and I have returned from the frontlines and do we have stories to share! Of Mud! Of Blood! and of Glory! Who would care to join me in the first week of September at the Frog and Peach for carousing and stories to share?"

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "It's been so long since I have set foot inside this city! *grumbles as he pays back 200 crowns in loans*"

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "My first week back, I have some important business to attend to.. my mistress must be quite lonely. I will be at the Frog and Peach the remaining three weeks though, drinking to being hale and hearty!"
