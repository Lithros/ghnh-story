# Winter 1627–28 — The Baronets' Raid on the King

*December 1627 – February 1628 · resolved at the table 4–5 November 2021 · Mission: Field Operations · Theater: the Norman bocage, on the road to Paris*

---

## The situation

With La Rochelle sealed under siege after the autumn's fighting, the army marched home — and straight into an invasion aimed at the person of the King himself. On the night of 4 November the Narrator posted "Processing the campaign tonight," and just after midnight opened the winter books:

**Narrator** — "The Winter Campaign"

**Narrator** — "With the Huguenots contained in La Rochelle, the bulk of the army is pulled back to Paris to defend the capital. This is fortuitous, as even with the English navy held off at sea, and Buckingham in chains, a new English threat has presented itself.

A swarm of new baronets, created by Charles V to bolster the officer corps of the military, have taken it upon themselves to seize King Louis himself. Landing in Normandy, the new lords and their household forces have made quick work of the meager northern coastal defenses and set inland. Thankfully, they lack common leadership, and have split up across the bocage to pillage and raid on their way to the capital."

**Narrator** — "With the Cardinal overseeing the siege at La Rochelle, the King himself has condescended to order the army to defend his royal person at all costs. This will be a Field Operation."

## The reckoning

### Jerome Greybeck — Subaltern, Frontier Regiment

**Narrator** — "@CitrusNotes, your rolls are Death 7, Mention 10, Promotion 7, Crowns 10."

*Greybeck's first death roll went out on the wrong dice:*

> 🎲 Jerome Greybeck rolls 1d6 Death — **2**

**Narrator** — "Jerome Greybeck survived!"

**CitrusNotes** — "Double checking himself for mortal wounds Jerome thinks he may have used the wrong dice notation"

**Narrator** — "Oh, yeah. Try again" — "2d6"

> 🎲 Jerome Greybeck rolls 2d6 Death — **12** (Death target 7: he dies)

**Narrator** — "Jerome Greybeck, eager to prove himself, leads the charge. His big moment has finally arrived. The biggest gamble of his life is about to pay off."

**Narrator** — "With his comrades dropping like flies around him, he manages to enter the hedgerow itself, only to be tangled in its vines. The treacherous Norman countryside has him in its grips. He makes easy pickings for the English bayonets."

*The player gave his man one last flourish:*

**CitrusNotes** — "Bleeding profusely and losing all sense, Jerome sees a small vine of ripe berries growing at his feet. One last meal, he thinks as he weakly scoops a few into his mouth. A final throw off the dice. They were, of course, poisonous, but you must have blood in your body for that to matter..."

### Primo Tremblay — Captain, C Troop, Princess Louisa's Light Dragoons

**Narrator** — "@Abelard Soucy, your rolls are Death 11, Mention 10, Promotion 9, Crowns 8."

*Primo had spent the autumn hiding in a scarecrow and the months since under a −4 SP cloud of disgrace. This winter he chose redemption:*

**Primo Tremblay** — "Primo, has taken to hard living for the past few months and practiced an ascetic regime after experiencing the loss of his lady love as well as his social standing due to his cowardice. Cowardice, which, he reasoned, came from soft city living. Now, hardened, and with his soul right with God, he takes Reckless Bravery for 2. He leads the Dragoons on, and is the first to dismount and engage an isolated pocket of enemies."

> 🎲 Primo Tremblay rolls 2d6 death — **9** (Death target 11, less 2 for Reckless Bravery: he dies)

**Narrator** — "Primo feels confident under such a seasoned commander. He holds his place aside his horse without the fear that has haunted him before. He is where he is meant to be. Even as the sharp crack of musket fire heralds the approaching enemy, he steadies his pistol and readies for the fight."

**Narrator** — "Primo's horse is less ready. As the volley of incoming fire spatters harmlessly into the ground before the waiting dragoons, his horse spooks, and turns to run. In the process, it catches a stirrup against Primo's sword, pulling him to the ground and dragging him behind into a field of waiting thistles."

**Narrator** — "What returns to camp, still dragged behind the horse, is not recognizable as a soldier or a man, but the grooms know the horse well."

*Two fresh tombstones went up in the cemetery channel within the hour — Greybeck's at 1:00 AM, Tremblay's at 1:08. By 1:16 AM CitrusNotes was already rolling 3d6 for his next man, the dice bot still answering under the dead man's name: "Jerome Greybeck: An error was thrown when evaluating your expression..."*
