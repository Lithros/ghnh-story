# Summer 1627 — The Flaming Galleon of Île de Ré

*June – August 1627 · resolved at the table 21–23 August 2021 · Mission: Field Operations · Theater: Île de Ré, off the Atlantic coast of France*

---

## The situation

Two years after the Frontier Regiments stormed its fortress, Île de Ré was again the hinge of the war — this time with the Duke of Buckingham himself standing on it. On the evening of 21 August the Narrator opened the summer books:

**Narrator** — "The summer heat will stir even the most languid foe into open conflict with the right-hearted favorites of God. The army is once again deployed to the fortress-island of Ré. The English, dissatisfied with the progress in the Huguenot rebellion they've so vigorously encouraged, and seeking to lift the siege on La Rochelle by any means necessary, have landed several thousand men on the Île de Ré. With the Duke of Buckingham himself commanding the English forces, they have been able to swiftly cover the grounds of the island generally, with only the star fort keeping them at bay. Buckingham has settled in for a siege, and only brave smugglers, incentivized by wise bounties from Cardinal Richelieu, have been able to keep the fortress supplied."

**Narrator** — "The army arrives as part of a rescue mission, seeking to break the siege and save the courageous defenders of Saint-Martin-de-Ré. The full might of the Royal Army crashing onto the shores ought to shatter the morale of any enemy, but Buckingham orders what defenders he can to go to ground, spreading themselves over the island to fight man-to-man where necessary."

**Narrator** — "All units are performing Field Operations."

*In the OOC channel that same night, the mood was set in one line:*

**etirflita** — "We're going to make the English go down so hard!"

## The record

**Narrator** — "Gen. Vaganay has tussled with Buckingham before. He surveys the map, and compares it to the marshy ground stretching before him. It's mid-morning before the Royal forces are finished disembarking and sufficiently organized to begin the attack. The explosions of cannon echo over the waves from the sea battle taking place just off the island."

**Narrator** — "If Saint-Martin is to be saved, it will require all his arts. He summons his division commanders. The 1st Division will stay in reserve, to reinforce where needed. The 2nd Division will make up the main attack force, spreading out to search and destroy the enemy. The Cavalry Division will range ahead, spotting and engaging only where necessary. The Dragoon Brigade will be sent in as a surgical strike force, seeking to circle around and cut off the English forces in the field from their command camp by the siege zone."

> 🎲 Gen. Vaganay's command roll: 1d6 — **2**

**Narrator** — "The orders are textbook. The battlefield is not. The cavalry finds itself terribly slowed by the marshy ground. Without their intelligence, the infantry are set upon from all sides as they advance, though at least they come expecting it. The result is Inconclusive (BR 4)."

### Honors, plunder, and the aftermath in Paris

Not one player character died on the Île de Ré — the first campaign in recent memory to add no new tombstones. The season's ledger: **Sir Marius Thibodeaux** knighted, mentioned in dispatches, +1 MA, and 400 crowns in captured gold medals; **Curtis Sinnoch** created **Baron**, +1 MA, and 500 crowns; **Robert d'Engaux** confirmed as a full Lieutenant Colonel; **Jules Lavelle** mentioned in dispatches (+1 SP forever) and 400 crowns richer; **Jean Renault** carrying home a 500-crown relic he would refuse to ever sell.

*Back in Paris, the veterans met in the streets to compare stories:*

**Jean Renault** — "I got separated from the main forces, did everyone make it home alive from this summer's campaign?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "Of course. The English are a laughable foe. Though they did fight with courage, I will admit."

**Jean Renault** — "Indeed, to the point of insanity I may add" — "My men and I thoroughly thrashed them"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Indeed @Jules Lavelle - they can fight with courage. They are also foolhardy and dim-witted, and expert fire from our regiment drove them from the glorious field! Your men in particular did an admirable job ensuring none of the British made it to the surf - excellent marksmanship."

**Jules Lavelle** — "Thank you, sir. It was easier than stabbing bilge rats in beggar's alley back home."

**Primo Tremblay** — "Mon Dieu, Paris can't talk about anything but the War! I must find my way into a better regiment if I'm to have any hope of securing my family's future!"

**Sir Marius Thibodeaux** — "The Royal Marines are always looking for fresh meat.. I mean, fresh men!"
