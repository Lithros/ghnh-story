# Spring 1627 — Chevreuse: The Campaign of Three Graves

*March – May 1627 · resolved at the table 30 July 2021 · Mission: Assault · Theater: the town, fields, and château of Chevreuse, two days' march from Paris*

---

## The situation

The enemy this spring was not across the Channel but in the very heartland — English gold and a hooded old nemesis stirring treason among the French nobility. The Narrator set the board:

**Narrator** — "The English are at it once more. The filthy sons of Albion spread their vile, corrupting touch across the very heartland of France. The English have sent the provacateur Walter Montagu to stir dissent among the nobility against the French monarchy. He has raised funds and commitments of soldiers to support a new force to be commanded by France's old foe, the Duke of Soubise himself."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **3**

**Narrator** — "Loyalists among the manor staff of Claude, Duke of Chevreuse (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lorrain.jpg), have reported to the Cardinal's agents of a concentration of forces and supplies on his very lands a mere two days' march from Paris."

**Narrator** — "Chevreuse's castle, the Château de la Madeleine, is a fortified hilltop redoubt with a military tradition over 500 years old. Despite its age, it has been consistently maintained and modernized, and the walls are reinforced to withstand cannonades."

**Narrator** — "Although the castle is well-protected and will require a siege, the men and matériel intended for the war effort are scattered throughout the town and countryside of Chevreuse, and therefore vulnerable to Assault."

**Narrator** — "The Royal forces arrive from the north-east. There will be fighting in both town and field, to be sure. The enemy was alerted shortly after the army left Paris, and has lined the hills and fields with hastily-assembled earthworks and their accompanying trenches. The town of Chevreuse, too, has been fortified, with blockades and traps lining the streets, ready to harass and slow any advancing force."

## The aftermath

**Narrator** — "The Spring Campaign has concluded. Another town burned, another victory for France." *(🇫🇷)*

**Narrator** — "The castle surrenders immediately after the town is taken, without need for a siege. The Duke of Chevreuse successfully pins the blame on his wife, Marie de Rohan, claiming she went behind his back to work with Walter Montagu and Soubise. To the great fury of the Cardinal, Soubise is nowhere to be found in castle or town."

*Between 6:09 and 6:17 that evening, the Narrator posted three headstones to the cemetery channel, one after another — Pépin Bonheur (☁️×2), Hercule Bonaparte (💍×2), Henri Toulouse (🥷×2). Three player characters dead in a single victorious afternoon; no campaign of the war had cost more. Back in Paris, the survivors organized the wakes:*

**Robert d'Engaux** — "Upon his return from campaign, Robert writes to @Jean Renault and @laiv.wire to invite them to toady at Red Phillips on the 1st week of the next month"
