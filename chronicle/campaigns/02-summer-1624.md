# Summer 1624 — The Great Campaign against the Croquants

*June–August 1624 · resolved at the table 31 August – 10 September 2020 · Mission: Field Operations & Defence · Theater: the Croquant rebel country*

---

## The situation

The Spring campaign had ended in catastrophe: the Frontier Regiments broken on their own battlements, and Guillaume d'Avergny dead in the first onslaught. On the evening of 31 August the Narrator set the stakes for the summer:

**Narrator** — "The frontier regiments have suffered a crushing defeat at the hands of the rebel leaders Donat and Barran. Their names are growing in popularity, and the rebellion threatens to spread as nearby peasantry wonder if they too can escape the ruinous tax burdens of the King."

**Narrator** — "The King charges the Minister of War to raise a great army to quell the rebellion once and for all."

*What, precisely, the peasants were rebelling over was only named aloud in the streets of Paris after the army came home — a salt tax:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "Where have you been, my good man! It's been all the news. Filthy Croquants, risen again, put down again. The same as it ever was. … Apparently fretting over tax issues, like common money-grubbing swine. A salt tax, of all the preposterous notions. They live without art, culture, soap, but ask for a bit of their salt and it's an uprising all at once."

## The reckoning

*Before the first personal roll of the night, the Narrator laid out the two ways a gentleman may tilt the odds — the moral machinery of every campaign to come:*

> **Narrator** — "If you choose poltroonery, you may add any number you feel necessary to the die roll needed for death. You then roll a d6 — on a 1 or 2, your cowardice escapes notice, but you miss the chance for plunder (promotion/despatches are rolled as normal). On a 3, 4, or 5, you are seen, and lose twice the modification you took in SP every month going forward until you are mentioned in despatches. On a 6, you are immediately dismissed from the regiment and may not apply to any regiment until you've been mentioned in despatches while in a Frontier Regiment. Your old regiment will never accept you again. You also suffer the above SP penalty."
>
> **Narrator** — "If you choose reckless bravery, you may subtract any number you wish from the die roll needed for death. You will use that modifier on the die rolls for despatches, promotion, and plunder as well."

### Honors, casualties, and the ledger

**Narrator** — "The campaign has ended."

Four player characters were dead in a single night — Jean-Luc Aveline, Godefroy de Beaufosse, Etienne de Mercoeur, and Phillipe du Kaneda — the bloodiest single session of the entire game. Among the NPCs:

**Narrator** — "The commander of the Heavy Brigade, Bg. General Gilbert Roulet, is counted among the dead."

Four captains of the 53rd Fusiliers also fell, opening Leon Mandelbrot's path to a captaincy — a promotion with strings attached:

**Jules Lavelle (GM)** — "In order to keep your captaincy you are required to purchase a horse. This will cost 100 crowns, plus a monthly cost of 3 in feed and 2 to pay the groom"

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "I purchased a horse and have named him Joyeux"

**Guy de Cavoye** — "And they say chivalry is dead."

Pierre de Tête came home mentioned in despatches, 50 crowns richer, and solvent: "I managed to free myself of 250 crowns of debt during the summer campaign." He promptly announced in the streets: "Gentleman, your hero, recently returned from a successful campaign, will be hosting at the Frog and Peach the third week of this month. All gentleman are invited to attend as carousing is covered by my battle plunder!" — to which the new-minted Captain Mandelbrot replied, "I would love to join you, and share my own stories that got me a promotion and got many of my fellow fusilier's killed!"

### Aftermath in the streets of Paris

*The survivors and the newly rolled walked among the gossip. Leon could not stop talking about the plank:*

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "To those going to war, beware of wooden planks!"

**Jules Lavelle** — "What, did one fall on your foot when you were building fortifications?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "The other officers of the Dragoon Guard are all a-talk about the failures of their fellow dragoons on campaign. Was it really so dreadful as all that? I should hate to be associated with disappointments."

**Guy de Cavoye** — "From what I hear, blame it on the cavalry. Slipshod performance from what should have been a shining example of French military prowess."

**Leon Mandelbrot** — "Oh their attack was a tremendous debacle, though there were some notes of bravery"

*A muscular newcomer named Clemens Charron — harry's next character — wandered in knowing nothing:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "All this despondency. It was a great victory! The rebels were crushed!"

**Clemens Charron** — "Wait what … There was a rebellion?"

**Clemens Charron** — "I dunno, take a man's salt and you've taken so much from him - the flavor in his food, the sweat off his back, and one of the few luxuries in his cupboard."

**Jules Lavelle** — "I'm sorry? Are we affording mere peasants luxuries now? They should be grateful for whatever they can contribute to the greatness of France."

**Clemens Charron** — "My good sir, peasants are the backbone of France! I understand the reluctance to spend your valuable time among those most fragrant, garrulous, and course, but all men have their pleasures."

**Jules Lavelle** — "Villains are as villeins do, bear in mind."

**Clemens Charron** — "Perhaps a strong drink is in order?"

**Jules Lavelle** — "As always!"

*And Blastagarded's next character announced himself with a mission statement:*

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "A frail youth walks down the street. Unlike his peers, the cane he uses is more than just a fashion accessory. It is likely he could move without it, but clear its support gives him comfort. Despite his delicate appearance, his eyes gleam with the cold steel edge of a military mind hungry to be tested. Could this young man be what France needs to prevent the Summer of 1624 for happening ever again?"

*The next morning the Fall volunteer rolls went out — Grand Duke Max's Dragoons, the Queen's Own Carabiniers, and the Royal Marines all volunteered on 6s ("Bye Curtis," "Bye Pierre," "Bye Alphonse," said the Narrator) — though Pierre de Tête was plucked from the rolls when the Dragoon Brigade named him Brigade Major, while Jules Lavelle was chosen Brigade Major of the Horse Guards. The Croquant story itself ended some weeks later, on a scaffold in Paris:*

**Narrator** — "A crowd gathers in the square before city hall. The officers of the Cardinal's Guard arrived early in the morning to oversee the workers assembling the gallows. **Leon Mandelbrot** — "Their rampage killed my friends Phillipe du Kaneda and Jean-Luc Aveline!"
