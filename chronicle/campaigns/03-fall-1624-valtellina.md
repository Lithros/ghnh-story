# Fall 1624 — "My Life for France!": The Siege of the Valtellina Forts

*September–November 1624 · resolved at the table 26–27 September 2020 · Mission: Siege · Theater: the Valtellina, against the Spanish garrisons of the Alpine valley*

---

## The situation

The great army that crushed the Croquants had barely limped home when the fall levies went out. This was to be the first *volunteer* campaign of the game — no royal summons, only the luck of the regimental dice:

**Lithros (GM)** — "For the Fall season, rolling to see if any regiments or battalions are volunteered for duty. … they go on a 6. … this can be modified by influence, if desired"

Two and a half weeks later at the table — with the game clock run forward to November 1624 — the bill came due:

**Narrator** — "Preparing the volunteer campaign's resolution."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **4**

**Curtis Sinnoch (player)** — "awwww yisssssss"

**Narrator** — "The Frontier Regiment has been assigned to a Siege mission. They will attempt to capture the forts of Valtellina."

*With the assignment the Narrator posted an old engraving of a star-fort above the valley — Forte de Fuentes, "Parte verso Valtelino":*

![Forte de Fuentes, the fortress engraving posted with the siege assignment](../images/campaign/campaign_02_Forte_de_Fuentes.jpg)

*The stakes of that valley were only spelled out at the table much later, when the war returned there in 1626 — but the Narrator's explanation serves as well for 1624:*

**Narrator** *(at the return to the Valtellina, 1626)* — "Although the people of Valtellina themselves are Protestant, the Swiss Grison League has aligned itself with France to liberate the region from the hands of the Spanish and Papal armies occupying the valley. With Venice and Savoy also providing support, this is France's best chance to drive a wedge between the Habsburgs and maintain its strategic position in Europe -- a cause worth cooperating with a few Protestants over."

---

## The order of battle

The volunteer dice of early September had picked their victims on 6s: the **Queen's Own Carabiniers** ("Bye @Curtis Sinnoch," said the Narrator), **Grand Duke Max's Dragoons** ("Bye @Pierre de Tête"), the **1st Squadron of Princess Louisa's Light Dragoons**, and the **3rd Battalion of the Royal Marines** ("Bye @Alphonse Devereux").

**Narrator** — "Barring exerting Influence on their commanders, @Curtis Sinnoch , @Pierre de Tête , and @Alphonse Devereux will be campaigning in the Fall season"

Pierre de Tête escaped the rolls at once — "Grand Duke Max's Dragoons. @Pierre de Tête has been chosen for the position [of Dragoon Brigade Major]. … As such, he has been removed from volunteering for campaign." Jules Lavelle was likewise named Brigade Major of the Horse Guards, Léonce Masse of the Picardy Musketeers was chosen for the First Brigade "by virtue of being related to General Masse," and Gaël Houdin of the 53rd Fusiliers took the 2nd Brigade.

**etirflita (player)** — "This is for the entire season, n'est-ce pas?"

**Lithros (GM)** — "The people being volunteered are gone until December"

*Curtis Sinnoch, a Captain of the Queen's Own Carabiniers, refused to march as a mere line officer:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Well, Looks like I shall be heading into battle this Fall! I might as well use my tactical acumen and insight where it can best serve France! I shall see if the Colonel will have my as a Regimental Adjutant! (5+ roll needed, utilizing two class 3 favors to give myself a +2 to the roll, along with my SL giving me a +2 Bonus. I am looking at a 1 or higher.)"

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6+4 — (3)+4 = **7** (adjutancy secured)

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "For King and Country!"

*Alphonse Devereux, Captain in the Royal Marines, took the news with grace:*

**Alphonse Devereux** — "I would love to join you both, but I must take my turn at war."

*And Guy de Cavoye — a bastard son newly arrived in Paris, a subaltern in the Royal Foot Guards for exactly one week — volunteered himself:*

**Guy de Cavoye** — "After a week of duty I break the news to my commander that I will not be able to report for duty next month as I, @Guy de Cavoye, am volunteering to fight for my country!"

**Narrator** — "Col. Bouchard looks at @Guy de Cavoye as though he has lost his mind."

**Guy de Cavoye** — "One hopes he can find it on the battlefield.."

*From the front, in September, one letter home survives:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, my dearest Adelaide, how I miss you so. It is difficult on the fronts, but I know we shall be victorious in this campaign! Then I shall return, and we can be together once again!"

When the siege lines were drawn, the Narrator sorted the volunteers:

**Narrator** — "@Curtis Sinnoch , @Guy de Cavoye , and @Alphonse Devereux are fighting. Curtis serves under his regiment. Guy and Alphonse have been placed into the chain of command of the Frontier Regiment itself."

| Character | Rank / post | Serving with |
|---|---|---|
| **Curtis Sinnoch** | Captain & Regimental Adjutant | Queen's Own Carabiniers, under Col. Denis Nee |
| **Guy de Cavoye** | Subaltern (Royal Foot Guards), volunteer | chain of command of the Frontier Regiment, under Col. Rémy Aymard |
| **Alphonse Devereux** | Captain, Royal Marines 3rd Battalion | chain of command of the Frontier Regiment |

The two NPC colonels got their vignettes in a single breath:

**Narrator** — "The two regiments operate independently, with little communication. Col. Denis Nee, commanding the Queen's Own Carabiniers, is a leader of great foresight and reliability. He convinces the Frontier Regiment's commander, Col. Rémy Aymard, to conduct a frontal attack to try to break the siege, while the Q.O.C. scale the walls unnoticed."

## The record

### The Frontier Regiment at the gates

**Narrator** — "The defenses have been battered by weeks of blockade and cannonade, but the walls remain intact. The Frontier Regiment rushes toward the gates under heavy fire. 'My life for France!' screams Col. Aymard."

**Narrator** — "The frazzled, exhausted Spanish defenders may not understand the shout, but they know too well its meaning."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **3** (Col. Aymard's command)

**Narrator** — "Col. Aymard's orders are solid, but no less solid than the gates themselves. The attackers are repelled, but in good order, and return to their lines mostly intact. The outcome of the Frontier Regiment's role in the siege is Inconclusive."

*Guy de Cavoye's personal rolls followed at once — see The reckoning below.*

### The Queen's Own Carabiniers on the far wall

**Narrator** — "As the fighting against the gate reaches its peak, around the far side of the fort, Col. Nee gives the order for his men to deploy grapnels and begin their ascent."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **5** (Col. Nee's command)

**Narrator** — "The men throw with all their might, but there are few defenses at this part of the wall, and therefore little purchase. A few defenders throw rocks, but there aren't enough targets on the wall for it to be very dangerous. After thirty minutes of fruitless effort, Col. Nee calls off the attack."

**Narrator** — "The Queen's Own Carabiniers' role in the siege is Inconclusive."

*Two assaults, two shrugs from the dice. The forts did fall in the end — the December Gazette de France ran the campaign's epitaph in its Military News column, the most honest sentence it ever printed:*

**Gazette de France, Novembre 1624** — "News from the front: the Frontier Regiment was triumphant at Valtellina, once the defenders ran out of food."

![Gazette de France, Novembre 1624](../images/gazette/gazette-de-france_06_1624_Novembre.png)

### Honors, plunder, and the road home

The ledger of the fall: no player character dead, one Q.O.C. captain fallen, Alphonse Devereux Mentioned in Despatches with two hundred crowns of stolen heraldry in his baggage, and nothing at all for the men at the gates. Back in the streets of Paris, when Jules Lavelle asked aloud whether anyone might loan him 240 crowns:

**Guy de Cavoye** — "Alas, the fighting was not as lucrative as I might have hoped."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Ah, to be back in the grand city of Paris, with my pockets flush with cash after several months not spending anything on that damn campaign! I'm sure I was this close to a promotion or other notice, but c'est la vie! To be home once more, and to LIVE!"

**Alphonse Devereux** — "Hear, hear!"

**Alphonse Devereux** — "@Curtis Sinnoch, I would love to catch up with you after this campaign to trade tales. Would you care to host at Hunter's this month?"

*There was no rest in it. Within the same sitting the Narrator was already rolling for the Winter Campaign — "Royal Foot Guards are going on campaign. Bye @Guy de Cavoye" … "53rd Fusiliers are going. Bye @Leon Mandelbrot and @Henri Toulouse" — and Guy, twice a volunteer now, saluted:*

**Guy de Cavoye** — "At least I shall be paid my regiment's salary this time!"
