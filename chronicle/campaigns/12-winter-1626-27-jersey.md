# Winter 1626–27 — Jersey: "The Oily Surface Ignites"

*December 1626 – February 1627 · resolved at the table 11–12 July 2021 · Mission: Assault · Theater: the island of Jersey — the beaches, woods, and docks of Saint-Hélier*

---

## The situation

The winter of 1626–27 found France with no war to fight — and one insult it could not swallow. The Narrator laid out the strategic picture on the Sunday evening of the resolution:

**Narrator** — "It is a time of relative peace. The Spanish and Dutch are occupied with each other. The Savoyards and Genoese have learned their lesson, the Holy Roman Empire is riven with internal conflict, and the strength of the Huguenots has been broken."

**Narrator** — "Only one foe threatens the security of France. From across the Channel, the heirs of Guillaume le Conquérant create trouble for God's favored nation." *(three 😩 reactions)*

**Narrator** — "Under the thinnest of pretexts, the English have seized certain French naval vessels tasked with patrolling coastal areas still rife with Huguenot sympathy."

**Narrator** — "The King, furious, gives an ultimatum to the Cardinal. The Cardinal interprets the order, and makes it known to the Minister of War: there will be no official declaration of hostilities."

**Narrator** — "Nevertheless, it is understood that some reaction is required, or else France will seem utterly weak. Still, no open conflict with English military forces can be permitted. The Minister settles on a softer target."

*The soft target was Jersey — an unofficial reprisal raid against a fishing town, by a strike force carrying seven player characters, the largest deployment of the war so far. The Narrator opened the evening with the campaign roll:*

**Narrator** — "The Winter Campaign"

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **3**

**Narrator** — "This will be an Assault campaign."
