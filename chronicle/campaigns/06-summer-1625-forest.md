*June – August 1625 · resolved at the table 8–10 November 2020 · Mission: Field Operations · Theater: the forests of Western France, outside Nantes*

Nantes had fallen in the spring; the Huguenots now held the whole Atlantic seaboard. On the Sunday evening the Minister of War drew up the summer's deployment, and the table learned that nobody was staying home:

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **6**

**Narrator** — "Field Operations for everybody!"

**Jean Rochant** — "YAY! WE ALL GET TO DIE!"

*The Narrator set the strategic scene the next night:*

**Narrator** — "The Huguenot forces have spread throughout Western France, controlling the coastline north of Aquitaine. Even Bordeaux has fallen after the disastrous Spring Campaign."

**Narrator** — "With the French Navy mostly wiped out in the Winter Campaign, Cardinal Richelieu has made serious concessions to England and the Dutch in return for assistance against their Protestant brothers, in the form of loaned ships to be manned by French crews."

**Narrator** — "As Vice-Admiral Van Dorp roams up and down the Atlantic coast, seeking to engage the Protestant Navy, the Field Army of France has been deployed to root out and eliminate the scattered Protestant militias which have taken hold of the coast."
