# Fall 1627 — The Battle of the Scarecrows

*September – November 1627 · resolved at the table 28 September – 4 October 2021 · Mission: Field Operations · Theater: the Aquitaine farmland around besieged La Rochelle*

---

## The situation

The Marquis-Colonel of the Royal Foot Guards announced the season himself, a week before the books opened:

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Fine Gentlemen of Paris!  Spend an extra night with your mistresses, and all Subalterns are excused of duty!  (The Privates will cover for anything necessary).  WE GO TO WAR THIS MONTH!"

*On 28 September the Narrator rolled a 6 for the campaign — "This will be a Field Operations campaign" — and laid out the treachery at La Rochelle:*

**Narrator** — "After suffering miserable defeat under the leadership of Duke Soubise, the city of La Rochelle was thought to have learned its lesson, and become an ardent bastion of the Royalists. However, under pressure from the vile Buckingham and his fleet's blockade, and the besieging Huguenot army, the city's masters have broken down, opening the gates to let in Buckingham and his cohort.

Cardinal Richelieu himself has come to direct the siege efforts. With the bulk of the regular army devoted to the siege, the Cardinal has directed the Minister of War to allocate certain regiments to support the Frontier Regiment in scouring the countryside for lingering Huguenot forces bent on breaking the siege."

**Narrator** — "Although the Huguenots are scattered across the landscape, they are currently well-supplied and moving in cohesive military units. With their leadership holed up in La Rochelle, the rebels' hopes lie entirely on the English-supported field units meeting the Royal Army in open battle and emerging victorious to lift the siege."

**Narrator** — "Royalist forces will be attempting to seek-and-destroy the enemy over eighty square miles of wide-open Aquitaine farmland."

**Primo Tremblay** — "Primo was certainly not about to disabuse him of that notion. He shined his shoes, straightened his cap, decorated it with a lucky feather from Armadine's cock, the Right Reverend Raphael Legoinegasque, and marched to the regimental house to apply and purchase a captaincy while the wind blew in his favor."

## The record

**Narrator** — "As the ranking officer in the field, @etirflita will detail the plan of deployment and roll."

**Sir Marius Thibodeaux** — "The Royal Marines will be aiding in the siege effort to prevent the rejoining of the rebels and their leaders while the Picardy Musketeers will be systematically hunting the rebels in the nearby farmland."

*And, to the gentlemen back in Paris, from the streets that same hour:*

**Sir Marius Thibodeaux** — "We shall be back in time to Christmas in Paris. I plan to toast to France with you fine chaps next month!"

> 🎲 Bg. Gen. Thibodeaux's command roll: 1d6 — **6**

**Narrator** — "The 1st Brigade immediately makes contact with multiple enemy regiments. The Picardy Musketeers engage in pitched battle, and even manage to draw attention from the Royal Marines, who succeed in withdrawing to assist the siege. The Marines arrive just in time to rebuff a desperate sally by the defenders, successfully capturing George Villiers, the Duke of Buckingham himself, in the midst of an escape attempt."

**Narrator** — "The result is 1 - Enemy crushed."

*Three French flags went up in the reactions. Then the Narrator turned to the Gascons, and the campaign found its name:*

**Narrator** — "Maj. Servais Poussin, stolid and unpretentious commander of the Gascon Regiment's 3rd Battalion, leads his men with grim determination. Spotting a line of enemy forces across a field of asparagus, he orders a charge."

> 🎲 Maj. Poussin's command roll: 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "The enemy line is revealed to be a line of tactically-placed scarecrows. The 3rd Battalion opts to vent their frustrations on them anyway, screaming and bayoneting the silent guardians. They little notice the approach of a Huguenot force, who open fire from a mere 30 yards away, tearing huge holes in the line and sending the battalion scattering. The result is 6 - Own crushed."

*The Dragoons' colonel saw the massacre from a distance and turned his regiment:*

**Robert d'Engaux** — "Robert spots Poussin being ambushed and decides to intervene. His dragoons had been behind the 3rd battalion on their way to assist the siege, but Robert decides to take the opportunity to charge into the enemy while they are distracted."

*Meanwhile the Frontier Regiment got its orders:*

**Narrator** — "Col. Melvin Duret orders the Frontier Regiment into an encircling maneuver. Encircling what exactly, he is not sure."

> 🎲 Col. Duret's command roll: 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "The Frontier Regiment spreads out in a horseshoe shape throughout the farmland, thinly enough that they fail to encounter more than a few similarly disorganized enemy units. The result is 4 - Inconclusive."

*Robert's rescue charge came to the roll:*

> 🎲 Lt. Col. d'Engaux's command roll: d6 — **3**

**Narrator** — "By the time the Dragoons reach the 3rd Battalion, it is much too late. Scarecrows and soldiers are scattered like chaff, rather inappropriately for an asparagus field. The remaining Huguenots disperse, but the Dragoons are demoralized by their failure to save the footmen. The result is 4 - Inconclusive."

*The Royal Foot Guards took the field last, their Marquis-Colonel having drawn his own conclusions from the day's botany:*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "After hearing of the dangers of Asparagus (And silently commenting that he would still prefer the stink of a Parisian back alley in Springtime then the odor of the English), Curtis deploys his men along a forest ridge following a well-traveled route, hoping to catch a caravan of Huguenots as they re-position themselves"

> 🎲 Marquis-Colonel Sinnoch's command roll: 1d6 — **3**

**Narrator** — "The Royal Foot Guards, disciplined as ever, maintain their positions and pick off the enemy at a distance wherever he positions himself. Charge after charge of Protestant valor is met and repulsed by the implacable force of Marquis-Colonel Sinnoch and his regiment. The result is 2 - Enemy driven from field."

*And the 1st Battalion under Jules Lavelle — whose choice of metaphor, given what had just happened to Private Lefebvre (see below), was in exquisitely poor taste:*

**Jules Lavelle** — "This, truly, is no more difficult than a grouse hunt at my family's estate in Brittany. I can simply lean back and let my skillful soldiers do what they do best."

> 🎲 Lt. Col. Lavelle's command roll: 1d6 — **6**

**Narrator** — "True to form, Jules continues the fine example set by his commander. The result is 2 - Enemy driven from field."

## The reckoning

**Jerome Greybeck** — "The glass buttons that were the eyes of the scarecrow reflect a glint of light as the weapons of the forces were drawn and Jerome manages a quick duck and roll to take up better position"

> 🎲 2d6 — **5** (Mention 7) — **Narrator**: "No mention."

**Jerome Greybeck** — "Though, he survived, his actions were nothing to write home about. At least he gets the chance to fight another day"

> 🎲 2d6 — **6** (Promotion 6) — **Narrator**: "Promoted!"

### Sébastien Lefebvre — torn apart by grouse

**Narrator** — "@WillWorker, your rolls are Death 7, Mention 12, Promotion 8, Crowns 10"

**Sébastien Lefebvre** — "Unlike his compatriots, Sebastien knows how to hunt in a farmers field.  Keeping low to the ground and ears perked, he slowly moves towards the sound he's recognized since childhood, that of cooing grouse.  Avoiding disturbing the flock, he raises his rifle and thinks to himself, 'Tonight, I will eat well.'"

> 🎲 2d6 — **10** (Death 7: dies)

**Narrator** — "The grouse is finished. But its flock has long grown accustomed to the sound of gunfire, and what it means for their prospects for a meal in the midst of a battlefield. They sprint towards the sound of the shot, in great numbers. As Sebastien smugly observes the fallen bird, another of its kind pops its head out of the underbrush, softly cooing the last sound he will ever hear. His final thought, before being torn to shreds, is 'petite futée…'"

*The reactions tell the story: three doves, three crying-laughing faces, two stars, two screams, and a plank. A tombstone went up in the cemetery within the half hour. Sébastien's player spent the rest of the resolution haunting the survivors' rolls as a ghost — see below.*

### The close of the campaign

**Narrator** — "The battle is a great success, forcing the final field elements of the English-supported Huguenots back into the city. La Rochelle remains under siege, but with Cardinal Richelieu directing the construction of great siege engines, a firm naval blockade, and plans for further fortifications already in motion, the city's fall is only a matter of time."

**The ledger of Fall 1627.** The Duke of Buckingham — George Villiers himself — a prisoner of the Royal Marines. Sir Marius Thibodeaux: mentioned in despatches (+5 SP for 3 months, +1 thereafter), a 400-crown ducal ring off Buckingham's finger, and a promotion with nowhere to go. Curtis Sinnoch: mentioned, and 800 crowns of caravan goods. Jules Lavelle: mentioned, 700 crowns, and a barony. Jerome Greybeck: promoted Subaltern out of the wreck of the scarecrow charge. Primo Tremblay: alive, disgraced, at −4 SP a month. The Gascon 3rd Battalion: crushed among the asparagus. Dead: Pvt. Sébastien Lefebvre, of grouse; Lt. Col. Robert d'Engaux, of principle.

*The replacements were rolling in the chargen channel before the day was out. Robert's player introduced two arrivals:*

**sirsfurther** — "Two Fresh arrivals in Paris: one a wealthy heiress from Normandy, Marie d'Rouen (SL:15)" — "The other, the bastard spawn of the Earl of Ambroise and a washerwoman, Louis Laveuse"

*And the next day, in the streets en passant, Sébastien's player unveiled his own successor at the late Hercule Bonaparte's manor — one Jean Shawinigan, a stranded time traveler making the best of the seventeenth century:*

**Jean Shawinigan** — "So instead Jean Shawinigan will arrive in Paris and do the things young rich men do.  Calculations are that the damage to the timeline will be minimal.  But maybe I should damage the timeline?  Tough choice: Life stuck as a wealthy gentleman in the 1600s or stuck in a temporal prison in the 2800s?"
