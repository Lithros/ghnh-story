# Winter 1629 — The Ambush on the Creuse (the Last Campaign)

*February – March 1629 · resolved at the table 5 – 10 June 2022 · Mission: Assault · Theater: the royal highway along the river Creuse*

---

## The situation

*The seed of the season was planted a month early, on 13 May, when the orders call went out — and with it a very large box.*

**Narrator** — "@everyone orders due for February. If you want to volunteer for the Winter Campaign, now is the time."

**Albert Blastagarde** — "Having received his orders, Albert kisses his new beau farewell as his carriage is loaded with new prototypes.  The final large box which required four men to lift it bears the simple marking, 'Le Vaporisateur'"

*On 5 June 2022 the announcements channel posted "February 1629," and the campaign channel opened its final file:*

**Narrator** — "The Winter Campaign"

**Narrator** — "The Minister of War is especially pleased to learn that @everyone has volunteered!"

**Narrator** — "Henri, Duke of Rohan, a fiendish Huguenot leader, has learned nothing from the fall of La Rochelle and the terrible suffering the Protestants brought down upon themselves there. Seeing the folly of resisting Royal forces at sea, he's refocused his efforts inland, marching several armies overland at great speed to reach Huguenot strongholds in the South of France. One royal highway, running alongside the river Creuse, has been identified as the most likely avenue of escape for Rohan's remaining forces.

The Frontier Regiment and volunteers have been deployed to intercept, and hopefully destroy, the Huguenots before they can return to their bases of support in the South."

![The river Creuse — the photograph posted with the campaign briefing](../images/campaign/campaign_12_unknown.png)

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **3**

**Narrator** — "This is an Assault campaign."

## The order of battle

**Narrator** — "Bg. Gen. @Curtis Sinnoch commands the 1st Brigade of Foot, including Cpts. @CitrusNotes and @WillWorker with the Picardy Musketeers, led by Maj. Ghislain Maitron.

Lt. Col. @Jules Lavelle leads the Royal Foot Guards.

The Frontier Regiment, led by Col. Lucien Lièvremont, a seasoned and celebrated officer, has awarded temporary commissions to Lt. Col. @ArcticFox and his Crown Prince Cuirassiers 1st Squadron, as well as Cpt. @DancingHorse. Sub. @Slade Imbee and Pvt. @sirsfurther fill the ranks."

*In character terms: Brigadier General Curtis Sinnoch in overall command of the 1st Brigade of Foot; Captains Armand Dufour and Albert Blastagarde in acting command of the Picardy Musketeers' 1st and 3rd Battalions (the entire command staff having died at La Rochelle); Lieutenant Colonel Jules Lavelle with the Royal Foot Guards; Lieutenant Colonel Jean Renault's cuirassier squadron, Captain Abelard Soucy, Subaltern Slade Imbee, and Private Joseph Bousain all attached to Colonel Lièvremont's Frontier Regiment. Every player character in the game marched.*

## The record

**Narrator** — "Reconnoitering cavalry report the Huguenots en route as expected. Bg. Gen. Sinnoch and Col. Maitron meet the evening before the armies will make contact to draw up the plan of battle. Over multiple servings of wine, Col. Maitron suggests a pincer movement, with his diverse forces on the right, and the 1st Brigade on the left. He imagines it will drive the enemy into the river."

**Narrator** — "@Curtis Sinnoch, please give your orders and roll."

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "Col. Maitron, I agree our best bet is to force the craven Huguenots towards the river is one of our best bets.  However, I wish to make sure we utilize our best assets to ensure we inflict as much pain and suffering upon our foes as possible.  To that end, I wish to use our Cavalry along the road to harass our foes long before the actual battle commences, hitting and running, drawing our their lines while the Frontier Regiment sabotages supply lines.

We will launch our final attack here, where the river is shallow to the side closest to highway, but plunges unexpectedly towards the far bank.  We will line up the Royal Foot Guard just inside the treeline, poised to rip the enemy apart as they flounder in deeper waters.  What say you?"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "@Jules Lavelle you have final discretion on how to deploy your troops along the water line, but I would recommend keeping a small contingent of sharp shooters available to engage in specialty targets as they introduce themselves.  Have them focus on the opposing bank to fire on incoming threats to allow the rank and file time to engage the masses.  Godspeed."

> 🎲 Curtis Sinnoch rolls 1d6 — **3**

**Narrator** — "Bg. Gen. Sinnoch's plan takes the disorganized enemy almost completely unawares. Already exhausted from a long march after a terrible defeat, and with nighttime raids and from Royal cavalry, and the resulting hunger, keeping everyone on edge, morale among the Protestant troops is at rock-bottom even before the bulk of the Royal forces present themselves.

Only a few battalions, personally commanded by Rohan himself, manage to organize themselves quickly enough to provide any sort of resistance. The river runs red with the blood of their comrades as the battle wears on. The result is 2 - Bloody victory."

*Then, unit by unit, the plan met the enemy. First the Picardy Musketeers:*

**Narrator** — "Col. Maitron, seeking sensible glory for his Musketeers, holds them back from the battle until victory is assured. As the enemy begins to break, he finally orders the charge."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **1**

**Narrator** — "His delayed charge is timed almost perfectly with the Royal Foot Guard's major volley. Perfectly arrayed in defilade as per standard tactics, the RFG have aimed high to decapitate any mounted officers. There is a special prize out for any round that takes Rohan himself in the head.

The RFG's best marksman, spotting the dour Duke towards the rear, takes careful aim, lights his musket, and pulls the trigger. At just that moment, the Picardy Musketeers leap onto the hill, taking dozens of balls that had been intended for the enemy in their own backs instead. The Musketeer assault collapses, and the RFG rush to help their fallen allies.

The result is 5 - Repulsed."

**Jules Lavelle** — "A shambles! Bg. Gen. Sinnoch's perfect plan -- ruined! Forget this nonsense, men! Leave those Musketeer fools to their fates and follow me, for the GLORY of FRANCE! CHAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAARGE!"

> 🎲 Jules Lavelle rolls 1d6 — **1** *(the table reacts with a plank)*

**Narrator** — "Jules Lavelle charges forward on his horse, not taking the time to look back. His men, who had settled in for a comfortable, long-range engagement, are taking their time preparing for a charge. This is time Lavelle himself has not taken. Realizing his mistake, he turns his horse to rush back to his own lines, bellowing at his men to get to their foot.

Lavelle's horse trips over the corpse of a slain Musketeer, and they both go down in a surprised tumble. Worried for their commander, the mass of Guardsmen toss aside their arms and rush out to help -- just in time for the Duke of Rohan's small cavalry contingent to rush in from the side, sowing chaos and bloody slaughter among the confused Guardsmen.

Lavelle can do nothing but watch as the flower of French chivalry, entrusted to his care, is cut down before his very eyes. The result is 5 - Repulsed."

**Narrator** — "Col. Lièvremont plays it by-the-book as usual, securing ground and enforcing his flanks before advancing. The Frontier Regiment encircles the foe and steadily pushes him back into the river, where the Protestants, feeling their boots getting wet, panic."

> 🎲 Narrator rolls 1d6 — **1** *(another plank)*

**Narrator** — "But there is only one thing more dangerous than a loose wolf, and that is a cornered wolf. With certain death at their backs, the enemy fight like demons, and the Frontier Regiment lacks the cohesion to present any kind of solid resistance to an actual counter-assault. The volunteers and penal battalions begin dropping like flies, and the military reality of plans and contact with the enemy rears its ugly head once more. The plan hasn't survived, and neither will most of the Royal forces, as the Protestants cut a bloody swathe to freedom.

The result is 5 - Repulsed."

*As acting battalion commanders, Dufour and Blastagarde were each invited to give orders and obtain a personal battle result. Blastagarde's order was the one the whole war had been building toward:*

**Albert Blastagarde** — "While Albert's men may have been cut down by miscoordinated friendly fire, at the very least they completed their assigned task and positioned 'Le Vaporisateur' with an unobstructed view of the enemy.

The monstrosity of six full barreled cannons held together by an aiming mechanism was intimidating on its own.  But its true horror lay in its black powdered filled cannon balls designed to explode on impact... in theory.

Requiring four men to operate and one brave soul to be standing next to it when it fired, Albert was not about to let anyone else take the glory.

Albert orders the targeting of the most likely location of the Duke and, when ready, takes position beside his opus to fire it personally."

> 🎲 Albert Blastagarde rolls 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "The grotesque apparatus lets out a terrible scream of overstressed iron. Two of the balls explode in their barrels, but by some miracle of metallurgy, the tubes mostly contain the force, merely splitting apart at the ends. Three of the other four balls sail out into the mass of forces, missing Rohan and his small force, but impacting amidst the densest concentration of Protestant soldiery.

The effect is immediate and horrifying. Weapons, limbs, and surprised faces, some still attached to their heads, fly in every direction. Men who haven't bathed in weeks suddenly find themselves showered in a black liquid of mixed composition: one part river water, one part mud, and the rest the innards of their fellows.

The final ball goes high, soaring across the river entirely, to where the Royal cavalry are patiently waiting to cut down any survivors fleeing across the river. The ball fails to explode, but instead bounces along the ground, where it finds ample choice of horse legs to snap like brittle twigs in its passing. The screams of maimed horses fill the air wherever the screams of men go quiet.

The result is 3 - Pyrrhic victory."

*Dufour, grazed, trampled, and concussed, chose this moment for initiative:*

**Armand Dufour** — "Having been merely grazed in the back by stray bullets and only lightly trampled by confused horsemen, the VERY brief loss of consciousness caused by the shockwave of the experimental seige weapon was the final sign Armand needed. Clearly luck was on his side.

With this irrefutable message from on high, and not at all a concussion-induced fantasy, Armand quietly gathers the few remaining able-bodied members of his Battalion and steals through the underbrush to form a surprise attack at the rear of the foe. 'No one will expect us coming if we just slip in between them and the river!' He thinks, through a painful mental fog. 'Besides, I am dreadfully thirsty and could really use a rinse to investigate the blood stains on this uniform. Surely, they can't ALL be from me...'"

> 🎲 Armand Dufour rolls 1d6 — **4**

**Narrator** — "Despite the dangers and the battle frenzy of the pinned Protestants, there is simply no effective means of fighting on two flanks. As Rohan's troops are whittled down, the Musketeers begin suffering more harm from friendly bayonets than Protestant blades. The fighting is fierce, but over mercilessly quickly, even though only a few of the Royal forces are left standing.

The result is 3 - Pyrrhic victory."

*The last battle result of the war belonged to Jean Renault's cuirassiers, resolved two days later at the table (the Narrator by now posting from his player account — the voice is unmistakably the GM's):*

**Jean Renault** — "We will prepare an ambush down the highway, and see if we cannot route the advance!"

> 🎲 Jean Renault rolls 1d6 — **6**

**Narrator** — "Wisely ignoring the orders from the Frontier Regiment, Jean leads his cavalry down the road, where they engage a separate portion of the enemy force, peeling away a major section of reinforcements that might have turned the tide. Their sacrifice to distract the bulk of Rohan's might is heroic, but still a sacrifice -- no amount of maneuver or skilled riding can overcome the sheer numbers on the side of the Protestants, who nevertheless break when Rohan himself rushes back to order a withdrawal."

**Narrator** — "The result is 3 - Pyrrhic victory"

**Narrator** — "@ArcticFox, gain 1 MA for out-commanding your superior"

*Rohan escaped south. The battle was, on paper, a French victory; the field said otherwise.*

## The reckoning

### Albert Blastagarde — Captain, Picardy Musketeers (acting commander, 3rd Battalion)

**Narrator** — "@WillWorker, your rolls are Death 8, Mention 6, Promotion 7, Crowns 5."

> 🎲 Albert Blastagarde rolls 2d6 — **10** (Death target 8: dies)

**WillWorker** — "Worth"

**Narrator** — "The iron horror has done its work, and can now rest. But there is no rest for the makeshift scaffolding constructed to support it and assist its aim. Certain compromises had to be made to accommodate something so unwieldy that could also be readily turned, raised, and lowered.

Recognizing the unsteadiness of the prototype's base, Blastagarde had ordered it reinforced with long planks assembled into trusses on either side. His engineering accounted for the stress of firing, and they handled the force without any trouble. Just one of these planks was torn away by a splitting barrel. Just one was enough.

As Blastagarde surveys the carnage wrought by his creation, it teeters. His men's shouts of terror aren't enough to distract him from reveling in his success. His mind fills with visions of the transformation of warfare, of artillery becoming so powerful men can't move from trenches without being, as he puts it, vaporized.

The harbinger of the future slowly, implacably, inevitably descends on its inventor, who is, far from vaporized, flattened by his inability to get out of the way of progress."

*Three planks reacted to that one. A tombstone went up in the cemetery within the hour.*

### Joseph Bousain — Private, 13th Fusiliers, D Company

**Joseph Bousain** — "Bleary eyed from a wine soaked night with some of the other D company privates, Joseph finds himself marching near the back of the company, just in front of Cpt Matthieu Hachette, a man used to commanding from the back. Joseph grabs his victim and marches off into the forest to 'discipline' him, but instead leads him up to 'Le Vaporisateur' in the hope of being able to witness it in action.
