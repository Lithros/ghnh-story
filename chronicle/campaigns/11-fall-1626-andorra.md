# Fall 1626 — FOR SOUBISE! The Ambush in the Cabbage Fields of Andorra

*October–November 1626 · resolved at the table 16 April 2021 · Mission: Defence · Theater: the Principality of Andorra — the cabbage fields on the road to the capital*

---

## The situation

The fall season opened not with a war but with a peace. On the night of 16 April the Narrator posted **The Fall Campaign** *(the table's lone reaction: grim)* and laid out the most delicate errand the gentlemen were ever handed:

**Narrator** — "Cardinal Richelieu himself has embarked on a secret mission to negotiate a lasting peace with Spain. Fearing English perfidy, he orders the Frontier Regiment to accompany him as an honor guard on his journey to the Principality of Andorra, where he will meet with the Habsburg representative to discuss final terms."

*The escort had been assembling for weeks of game time. The Picardy Musketeers were volunteered clear back in February (real time), the moment the Rennes campaign closed:*

**Jules Lavelle (GM)** — "Picardy Musketeers 2nd Battalion Commander Maj. Augustin Ladagnous has decided to volunteer his unit for service in the Fall campaign. @DancingHorse will be deploying."

**Jean Rochant** — "@DancingHorse Right back into the fray then?"

**Marius Thibodeaux** — "I imagine you'll have a few sleepless nights on the front in the coming days"

**Guerinet Durand** — "Just when I was planning to use my newfound influence and wealth with the ladies!!"

*In April the rest signed on. Marius volunteered his portion of the Royal Marines for November — "Cool, I volunteer my portion of the Royal Marines" (🚀 musket plank) — and in the Royal Foot Guards' mess the officers talked themselves into it:*

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "Well Gentlemen, we have been kept from the fields of glory for far too long.  Let us go forth to remind our enemies what France's elite fighting force truly looks like."

**Jules Lavelle** — "Huzzah, back to the front where we belong, proving our mettle!"

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "I whole-heartily agree!  For the honor and glory of France!" — and, days later: "To war!" … "WAR!"

**Jean Rochant** — "The rest of us are at war, correct?" — "Come to war with us!"

*Days before the resolution — October 1626 in game — Jean Rochant paid 140 crowns for the Royal Marines' open lieutenant-colonelcy ("I will pay!"). He would command the 1st Battalion for exactly one battle.*

### Honors and the ledger

The Cardinal reached his conference table unscratched, and the rewards poured out of his coach almost as fast as the bullets had poured into it. **Hercule Bonaparte** took a Mention (+6 SP), a 400-crown treasury note pressed into his hand by Richelieu personally, and the campaign's grand prize: a **brevet Brigadier-Generalcy** and command of the **Dragoon Brigade** — a promotion he soon discovered was technically a step *down* in social precedence, prompting the Narrator's ruling that "there are perks to being the king's BFF." **Jules Lavelle**, who had ridden into the guns on three points of reckless bravery, converted an unavailable promotion into his second Mention (+3 SP), took the **Key to the City of Andorra** (300 crowns) from the Prince himself, and rolled a 6 against a 5 to become **Sir Jules Lavelle, Knight of France**. **Curtis Sinnoch** — one point from death — likewise turned an unavailable promotion into a Mention (+2 SP) and collected **600 crowns** in bounty for his battalion's kill count, plus **+1 MA** for outperforming his commander. **Guerinet Durand** took his 250-crown share of the Frontier Regiment's bonus home to Paris for the debauchery he had promised himself. **Marius Thibodeaux** survived, and got nothing else whatsoever.

And **Jean Rochant** — orphan, ex-private, cutlass-thrower, lieutenant-colonel of one month's standing — got the finest death narration of the war and a place in the necrology. In the days that followed the succession was settled in the off-season channel:

**Narrator** — "@Curtis Sinnoch, you are now the Acting Regimental Commander of the Royal Foot Guards."

**Hercule Bonaparte** — "@Curtis Sinnoch Don't redecorate the office just yet, but enjoy the chair.  I don't intend to be coming back, if it can be helped." *(plank ×2)*

**Curtis Sinnoch** — "@WillWorker I'll do my best - maybe bring in some fine cognac and restock the cabinet, but I'll leave a furniture alone!"

*Rochant's gravestone was posted to the cemetery five days later. The image drew three 🎣 reactions — a fish out of water to the last.*
