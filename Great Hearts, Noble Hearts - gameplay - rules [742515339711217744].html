============================= Order of Play: Prior to each new month, submit your actions for each of the month's 4 weeks. Players can engage in duels either by planning, or by choice if they happen to be in the same place and find a cause to duel. At the end of each month, players tabulate their incomes and expenses, both in money and status. While on campaign, players pay no expenses, but can take no actions. (edited)

============================= Status: At the end of each month, a character may increase his status level if he managed to obtain status points equal to 3 times the level sought. To go from level 1 to level 2, a character must earn 6 total points in a month. To go from level 5 to level 6, a character must earn 18 total points. A character cannot gain more than one level in a month (except by being granted a title). In order to maintain his current status level, a character must earn status points equal to his status level. Otherwise, he drops one. After calculating any change in status level, status points are wiped clean for the next month.
11:54 AM
============================= Support: At the end of each month, a character must pay twice his social level in crowns to cover his living expenses. Failure to do so results in losing one social level. Through conspicuous consumption, a character may spend three times his social level instead to earn an extra status point for the month. Support costs are evaluated based on the past month's social level, not it might change to for the next month. Conspicuous consumption may be purchased and applied at the end of the month, after all other actions have been calculated.

Narrator 10/16/2020 4:06 PM
=========================== New House Rule: Marriage After courting the same mistress for 6 months, a character of equal or greater Social Level to his mistress can use the Propose weekly action to ask for his mistress's hand. The player rolls a die. On a 4-6, his proposal is accepted. On a 2-3, it is rejected for the season. On a 1, the mistress will reject the proposal and refuse to consider future marriage proposals. A successful proposal results in an Engagement. A wedding party must be held by the end of the next season (paid for by the bride's family). The groom receives a dowry equal to 20x the mistress's SL (or 40x if she is Wealthy). The base support costs for a married character increases to 3xSL, and conspicuous spending becomes 4xSL, but the wife no longer counts as a mistress and that separate support cost is no longer paid. Wives do not benefit from their special traits while they are married. A married character receives a monthly +2 SP bonus as long as the marriage survives, and generates 1 additional Favour per season. A wife may be visited or taken to a club to satisfy the need for female companionship. Married characters can still pursue mistresses and gain the associated benefits as usual. Married characters who are caught committing an Indiscretion will find their base support cost increased to 4xSL for three months, and cannot rely on their wives for female companionship during that time. Any character may court another character's wife at a -2 penalty (and gains the benefits of her special traits, if any). A married character who discovers his wife cheating (after being successfully courted) immediately drops one Social Level and has Cause against the offending gentleman. Otherwise, the courtship process works the same as with mistresses. A courted wife grants +3 SP per month instead of the usual +1, and has no support cost. A married character cannot choose to volunteer for campaign. Wives can produce heirs. Rules to come.
